## What is accelerated TMS?

Accelerated TMS refers to any protocol that delivers TMS sessions faster than the standard once-daily, 5-days-per-week schedule. This includes compressed schedules (2-3 sessions per day over 2-3 weeks) up to intensive protocols like SAINT (10 sessions per day for 5 days).

## Why accelerate?

Standard TMS requires 36 daily sessions over 6-9 weeks. For many patients, this is a significant barrier:
- Time away from work (36 separate clinic visits)
- Childcare challenges
- Travel distance to TMS clinics
- Slow onset of benefit (weeks before improvement)

Accelerated protocols address all of these by compressing the same total treatment into a shorter timeframe.

## Types of accelerated protocols

### Compressed iTBS (2-3x daily)
- **Schedule:** 2-3 theta burst sessions per day, spaced 50+ minutes apart
- **Duration:** 2-3 weeks instead of 6-9
- **Evidence:** Multiple studies show comparable efficacy to standard pacing
- **Availability:** Increasingly offered at TMS clinics

### Twice-daily standard rTMS
- **Schedule:** Two 37-minute sessions per day (morning and afternoon)
- **Duration:** 3-4 weeks
- **Evidence:** Limited but promising
- **Availability:** Some clinics offer this

### SAINT-style intensive (5-day)
- **Schedule:** 10 iTBS sessions per day for 5 days
- **Duration:** 1 week
- **Evidence:** Strongest (79% remission in RCT)
- **Availability:** Limited to specialized centers

## Is faster as effective?

The available evidence is encouraging:
- The THREE-D trial showed theta burst (3 min) equals standard (37 min) per session
- Multiple studies of compressed schedules show no loss of efficacy vs standard pacing
- SAINT's 5-day protocol showed superior outcomes (though with higher total dose and precision targeting)
- The brain's neuroplastic response may actually benefit from closely spaced sessions

## Practical considerations

- **Fatigue:** Multiple daily sessions can cause more scalp discomfort and headache
- **Scheduling:** Need to block full days rather than brief daily appointments
- **Cost:** Usually the same total cost — just compressed into fewer calendar days
- **Insurance:** Most insurers cover the same number of total sessions regardless of pacing
- **Travel:** Accelerated protocols are ideal for patients traveling to a TMS center

## Who benefits most

- Patients traveling from out of town for TMS
- Those who can't take 6-9 weeks of daily appointments
- Patients who need faster onset of antidepressant effect
- People with scheduling constraints (work, childcare, school)
