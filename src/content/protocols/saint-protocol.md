## What is the SAINT protocol?

Think of it as TMS on fast-forward. The Stanford Accelerated Intelligent Neuromodulation Therapy (SAINT) protocol compresses an entire TMS treatment course into 5 consecutive days. Where standard TMS gives you 36 sessions spread over 6-9 weeks, SAINT delivers 50 sessions — 10 per day — in one work week.

Developed at Stanford University, it's the most aggressive TMS protocol available. And the results have turned heads.

## How SAINT differs from standard TMS

| Factor | Standard TMS | SAINT Protocol |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Total sessions | 36 | 50 |
| Daily sessions | 1 | 10 (with 50-min breaks) |
| Total duration | 6-9 weeks | 5 days |
| Targeting | Standard 5cm rule | fMRI-guided, patient-specific |
| Pulses per session | 600 (iTBS) or 3000 (10Hz) | 1,800 (modified iTBS) |
| Total pulses | 21,600-108,000 | 90,000 |

## The three innovations

1. **Accelerated dosing:** 10 theta burst sessions per day, spaced 50 minutes apart, tapping into the brain's reconsolidation window for enhanced neuroplasticity
2. **Precision targeting:** You get a functional MRI so clinicians can pinpoint the exact DLPFC subregion most strongly connected to the subgenual anterior cingulate cortex — a key depression hub in the brain
3. **Higher total dose:** 90,000 total pulses over 5 days. That's significantly more than standard courses deliver

## Clinical evidence

The numbers from the landmark 2022 randomized controlled trial (published in the American Journal of Psychiatry) are striking:
- **79% remission rate** vs 13% with sham. Read that again.
- **90% response rate** in the open-label phase
- Most people improved within the 5-day treatment period itself
- Effects held at 4-week follow-up
- Well tolerated, no serious adverse events

These are some of the best outcomes in the entire TMS literature.

## Who qualifies

SAINT is generally offered to people who:
- Have treatment-resistant major depressive disorder
- Have tried 2+ medications without enough relief
- Can commit to 5 consecutive full days (8+ hours per day at the clinic)
- Can undergo a brain MRI (no metal implants, no severe claustrophobia)
- Have no seizure disorder or other TMS contraindications

## The treatment experience

Here's what a SAINT week actually looks like:
- **Day 0 (pre-treatment):** Brain MRI for targeting, plus clinical assessment
- **Days 1-5:** You arrive early morning, then receive 10 iTBS sessions throughout the day with roughly 50-minute breaks between each one
- Each session lasts about 10 minutes
- Between sessions? Rest, read, eat, watch something. It's a lot of waiting.
- Most people describe increasing fatigue as the week goes on, but the discomfort stays manageable

## Where to find SAINT

SAINT is still rolling out, but availability is growing:
- **Stanford University** — where it all started
- Several academic medical centers offering "SAINT-like" accelerated protocols
- Specialized TMS clinics that have adopted the full protocol
- Not yet available at most standard TMS clinics

## Cost and insurance

Let's be direct about the money:
- **Cost:** $10,000-$25,000 (includes MRI, 50 sessions, clinical oversight)
- **Insurance:** Coverage is evolving. Some insurers cover it as "intensive TMS." Others deny it as experimental.
- **Prior authorization:** Expect more paperwork than standard TMS requires
- Ask the providing center about their insurance track record — they'll know which carriers have been cooperative

## Limitations

- Five full consecutive days is a real schedule commitment
- Requires a brain MRI (additional cost if not covered)
- Not yet available at most TMS clinics
- Long-term durability data is still being collected
- Real-world results may not match the clinical trial numbers — patient selection and protocol adherence matter
