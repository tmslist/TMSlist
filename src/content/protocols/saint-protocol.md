## What is the SAINT protocol?

The Stanford Accelerated Intelligent Neuromodulation Therapy (SAINT) protocol is an intensive TMS approach developed at Stanford University that compresses treatment into 5 consecutive days. Instead of the standard 36 sessions over 6-9 weeks, SAINT delivers 50 sessions (10 per day) in one work week.

## How SAINT differs from standard TMS

| Factor | Standard TMS | SAINT Protocol |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Total sessions | 36 | 50 |
| Daily sessions | 1 | 10 (with 50-min breaks) |
| Total duration | 6-9 weeks | 5 days |
| Targeting | Standard 5cm rule | fMRI-guided, patient-specific |
| Pulses per session | 600 (iTBS) or 3000 (10Hz) | 1,800 (modified iTBS) |
| Total pulses | 21,600-108,000 | 90,000 |

## The three innovations

1. **Accelerated dosing:** 10 theta burst sessions per day, spaced 50 minutes apart, leveraging the brain's reconsolidation window for enhanced neuroplasticity
2. **Precision targeting:** Each patient gets a functional MRI to identify the exact DLPFC subregion most strongly connected to the subgenual anterior cingulate cortex — a depression hub
3. **Higher total dose:** 90,000 total pulses over 5 days, significantly more than standard courses

## Clinical evidence

The landmark 2022 randomized controlled trial published in the American Journal of Psychiatry found:
- **79% remission rate** (vs 13% sham) — dramatically higher than standard TMS
- **90% response rate** in the open-label phase
- Rapid onset — most patients improved within the 5-day treatment period
- Effects sustained at 4-week follow-up
- Well tolerated with no serious adverse events

## Who qualifies

SAINT is generally offered to patients who:
- Have treatment-resistant major depressive disorder
- Have failed 2+ adequate medication trials
- Can commit to 5 consecutive full days of treatment (8+ hours per day)
- Can undergo a brain MRI (no metal implants, no severe claustrophobia)
- Have no seizure disorder or other TMS contraindications

## The treatment experience

A typical SAINT week:
- **Day 0 (pre-treatment):** Brain MRI for targeting, clinical assessment
- **Days 1-5:** Arrive early morning, receive 10 iTBS sessions throughout the day with ~50 minute breaks between sessions
- Each session lasts about 10 minutes
- Between sessions: rest, read, eat, watch content
- Most patients describe increasing fatigue through the week but manageable discomfort

## Where to find SAINT

SAINT is available at a growing number of centers:
- **Stanford University** (originating center)
- Several academic medical centers offering "SAINT-like" accelerated protocols
- Specialized TMS clinics that have adopted the protocol
- Not yet widely available at standard TMS clinics

## Cost and insurance

- **Cost:** $10,000-$25,000 (includes MRI, 50 sessions, clinical oversight)
- **Insurance:** Coverage is evolving. Some insurers cover as "intensive TMS." Others deny as experimental.
- **Prior authorization:** More documentation typically required than standard TMS
- Ask the providing center about their insurance experience and success rates with authorization

## Limitations

- Requires 5 full consecutive days — significant schedule commitment
- Requires brain MRI (additional cost if not covered)
- Not yet available at most TMS clinics
- Long-term durability data still being collected
- Real-world results may not match clinical trial numbers (patient selection, protocol adherence)
