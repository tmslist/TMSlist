## Why maintenance TMS matters

TMS produces lasting changes in brain circuits, but depression can recur. Studies show that 50-60% of TMS responders maintain their improvement at 12 months, but some patients experience symptom return. Maintenance TMS — periodic booster sessions after the initial course — may extend the duration of benefit.

## Who needs maintenance?

Not everyone who responds to TMS needs maintenance sessions. Factors that increase the likelihood:

- **History of recurrent depression** (3+ episodes)
- **Chronic depression** (continuous symptoms for 2+ years before TMS)
- **Incomplete remission** (responded but didn't fully remit)
- **Prior relapse** after previous TMS course
- **Discontinuation of antidepressants** after TMS

## Evidence for maintenance TMS

- A 2020 meta-analysis found maintenance rTMS significantly reduced relapse rates compared to no maintenance
- Patients receiving monthly maintenance sessions had 70% sustained response at 1 year vs 50% without maintenance
- The optimal maintenance schedule appears to be weekly sessions tapering to monthly over 3-6 months
- Some patients need only quarterly sessions; others benefit from ongoing monthly treatment

## Common maintenance schedules

**Taper protocol (most common):**
1. Weekly sessions for 4 weeks after completing initial course
2. Biweekly sessions for 4-8 weeks
3. Monthly sessions for 6-12 months
4. As-needed sessions if symptoms start returning

**Fixed schedule:**
- Monthly single sessions indefinitely
- Some clinics offer "cluster" maintenance (3 sessions over 1 week, quarterly)

**Symptom-triggered:**
- Patient monitors mood (PHQ-9 or daily rating)
- Books maintenance sessions when scores begin increasing
- Most patient-centered but requires good self-awareness

## Insurance coverage for maintenance

Coverage varies significantly:
- Some plans cover 6-12 maintenance sessions per year
- Others require re-authorization for each cluster
- Some plans don't cover maintenance at all
- Document clinical necessity with PHQ-9 scores showing symptom increase without maintenance

## Retreatment vs maintenance

**Retreatment** is a full new course (36 sessions) when symptoms have fully returned after a period of wellness.

**Maintenance** is periodic single sessions or small clusters to prevent relapse.

Retreatment response rates are generally high — 80-90% of patients who responded to their first course respond to retreatment.

## Practical tips

- Discuss a maintenance plan before finishing your initial course
- Track your mood weekly (PHQ-9 app or journaling) to catch early relapse signs
- Don't wait for full relapse — early intervention with a few booster sessions is easier than starting over
- Some clinics offer maintenance packages at reduced per-session rates
