## Beyond anatomy: targeting brain function

Standard TMS targeting uses anatomical landmarks — measuring 5cm forward from the motor cortex. fMRI-guided TMS goes further by mapping the functional connections in your individual brain to identify the most effective stimulation target.

## How fMRI-guided targeting works

1. **Resting-state fMRI scan:** While you rest in the MRI scanner, the machine captures patterns of brain activity over 10-15 minutes
2. **Connectivity analysis:** Software maps which brain regions are functionally connected — specifically the connection between the DLPFC and the subgenual anterior cingulate cortex (sgACC)
3. **Target identification:** The DLPFC subregion with the strongest negative correlation to the sgACC is identified as the optimal target
4. **Treatment delivery:** TMS is delivered precisely to this personalized target

## Why the sgACC connection matters

The subgenual anterior cingulate cortex is a key hub in depression neurocircuitry. Research by Fox, Pascual-Leone, and others found that the most effective TMS targets in the DLPFC are those with the strongest anticorrelation to the sgACC. This finding underpins the SAINT protocol's success.

## Evidence

- Multiple retrospective studies show that patients whose stimulation sites had stronger DLPFC-sgACC anticorrelation had better outcomes
- The SAINT protocol (which uses fMRI-guided targeting) achieved 79% remission — far above standard TMS
- A 2023 prospective study confirmed that fMRI-guided targeting improved response rates by approximately 15-20% over standard targeting

## Current limitations

- **Cost:** fMRI adds $800-$2,000 to treatment cost
- **Availability:** Requires an MRI center and specialized analysis software
- **Time:** Additional visit for scanning + processing time before treatment starts
- **Insurance:** fMRI for TMS targeting is rarely covered (the MRI itself may be covered separately)
- **Expertise:** Requires clinicians trained in functional connectivity analysis

## Where to find fMRI-guided TMS

- Academic medical centers with TMS research programs
- SAINT protocol providers (fMRI targeting is part of the protocol)
- Specialized precision psychiatry practices
- Growing availability as the approach becomes more standardized
