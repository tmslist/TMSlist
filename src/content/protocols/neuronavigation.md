## What is neuronavigated TMS?

Standard TMS uses the "5cm rule" — positioning the coil 5cm anterior to the motor cortex hotspot. This approximation works for most patients, but brain anatomy varies between individuals. The actual DLPFC target can be 1-3cm away from where the 5cm rule places the coil.

Neuronavigated TMS uses the patient's own brain MRI to identify the precise target, then tracks the coil position in real-time during treatment to ensure accurate stimulation.

## How it works

1. **Pre-treatment MRI:** Patient undergoes structural (and sometimes functional) brain MRI
2. **Target identification:** Clinician identifies the optimal DLPFC target on the patient's brain scan
3. **Registration:** The system creates a 3D model of the patient's head and brain
4. **Real-time tracking:** During treatment, cameras or sensors track the coil and patient's head position
5. **Feedback:** The system displays coil position relative to the brain target in real-time, allowing precise adjustment

## Evidence for better targeting

- **Stanford research (2021):** Found that targeting the DLPFC subregion most connected to the subgenual cingulate (identified by functional connectivity MRI) predicted treatment response
- **Precision matters:** A 2019 study showed that patients whose actual stimulation site was closer to the optimal target had better outcomes
- **Individual variation:** Brain folding patterns mean the same skull landmark can correspond to different brain regions across patients

## Systems available

- **Nexstim SmartFocus TMS:** Purpose-built navigated TMS with E-field modeling
- **Localite TMS Navigator:** Add-on neuronavigation system compatible with various TMS devices
- **BrainSight (Rogue Research):** Research-grade neuronavigation
- **ANT Neuro visor2:** Compatible with multiple TMS systems

## Who benefits most

- Patients who haven't responded to standard TMS (targeting may have been suboptimal)
- First-time TMS patients who want maximum precision
- Patients with atypical brain anatomy
- Research participants in precision psychiatry protocols
- Patients willing to undergo brain MRI for personalized targeting

## Cost and availability

- **Additional cost:** MRI ($500-$2,000) + navigation setup
- **Total treatment cost:** Typically $1,000-$3,000 more than standard TMS
- **Insurance:** MRI may be covered separately; navigation premium usually not covered
- **Availability:** Primarily at academic centers and specialized practices — not standard at most TMS clinics

## The future

Neuronavigation is likely to become standard as the field moves toward precision psychiatry. The SAINT protocol's success — largely attributed to fMRI-guided targeting — is accelerating adoption of navigated approaches.
