## What is theta burst stimulation?

Intermittent theta burst stimulation (iTBS) is a TMS protocol that mimics the brain's natural theta rhythm (5Hz) with rapid bursts of stimulation. Instead of delivering individual pulses at 10Hz for 37 minutes, iTBS delivers triplet bursts (3 pulses at 50Hz) repeated every 200ms, completing a full session in approximately 3 minutes.

## The science behind theta burst

The theta rhythm (4-8Hz) is associated with learning and memory consolidation in the hippocampus. iTBS replicates this pattern:
- **3 pulses at 50Hz** (a "burst") — mimics hippocampal sharp-wave ripples
- **Bursts repeated at 5Hz** (theta frequency) — mimics the theta oscillation
- **2-second trains** with 8-second pauses — intermittent pattern enhances long-term potentiation

This pattern is thought to be more efficient at inducing neuroplastic changes than standard 10Hz repetitive pulses.

## The THREE-D trial

The 2018 THREE-D trial (published in The Lancet) was the definitive study:
- **414 patients** randomized to iTBS vs standard 10Hz rTMS
- **iTBS was non-inferior** — equivalent antidepressant efficacy
- Response: iTBS 49% vs standard 47%
- Remission: iTBS 32% vs standard 28%
- Session time: 3 minutes vs 37 minutes
- Side effect profile: equivalent

This trial led to FDA clearance of iTBS protocols (NeuroStar Express TMS).

## Practical advantages

- **3-minute sessions** vs 37-minute sessions — transformative for patient convenience
- **Higher clinic throughput** — clinics can treat more patients
- **Better compliance** — patients more likely to complete full 36-session course
- **Same efficacy** as standard protocols per THREE-D trial
- **Enables accelerated protocols** (multiple daily sessions become feasible)

## Standard iTBS protocol

- **Pulses per session:** 600 (in triplet bursts)
- **Session duration:** ~3 minutes 9 seconds
- **Sessions:** 36 over 6-9 weeks (same schedule as standard TMS)
- **Target:** Left DLPFC (same as standard rTMS)
- **Intensity:** 120% of motor threshold (same as standard)

## Who should consider iTBS

- Any patient who would benefit from standard TMS (same indications)
- Patients with limited time (busy professionals, parents, students)
- Patients with long commutes to the TMS clinic
- Those who want to minimize time away from work

## Availability

iTBS is widely available — most modern TMS systems support theta burst protocols:
- **NeuroStar Express TMS:** FDA-cleared iTBS protocol
- **MagVenture:** Supports iTBS
- **BrainsWay:** Supports TBS protocols
- Ask your TMS clinic if they offer theta burst — many have transitioned from standard protocols
