## Veterans and TMS

Military veterans face disproportionate rates of depression, PTSD, and traumatic brain injury — conditions that are often intertwined and treatment-resistant. TMS is emerging as a valuable tool in veteran mental health care, offered at a growing number of VA medical centers and veteran-focused clinics.

## Why TMS for veterans

- **Treatment-resistant depression:** Many veterans have tried multiple medications without adequate relief
- **PTSD comorbidity:** Depression often co-occurs with PTSD; TMS can address both
- **Medication burden:** Veterans often take multiple medications; TMS adds no drug interactions
- **TBI-related depression:** Post-TBI depression may respond differently to medication but well to TMS
- **No sedation:** Important for veterans who need to drive, work, or care for families

## VA TMS programs

The VA has been a leader in TMS adoption:
- 20+ VA medical centers now offer TMS
- VA research has contributed significantly to TMS evidence, especially for PTSD
- Veterans enrolled in VA healthcare can access TMS through internal referral
- Community Care referrals available if local VA doesn't have TMS

## TRICARE coverage

Active duty, retirees, and dependents covered under TRICARE can access TMS:
- Prior authorization required
- 2+ medication failures documented
- Available at both military treatment facilities and civilian network providers

## TMS for veteran PTSD

While TMS is FDA-cleared for depression (not PTSD), research in veteran populations is promising:
- VA-sponsored trials targeting right DLPFC for PTSD symptoms
- Deep TMS studies for combat-related PTSD
- Some VA centers offer TMS for PTSD under research protocols
- Many veterans with PTSD also qualify for TMS under the depression indication

## Finding veteran-focused care

- **VA medical centers:** Ask your VA psychiatrist about TMS availability
- **Veteran-focused clinics:** Some private TMS practices specialize in veteran care
- **Clinical trials:** VA actively recruits for TMS studies (ClinicalTrials.gov: "TMS veterans")
- **Non-profits:** Organizations like the Steven A. Cohen Military Family Clinics offer mental health services including referrals for TMS
