## Veterans and TMS

Veterans carry a disproportionate burden of depression, PTSD, and traumatic brain injury — conditions that tangle together and resist standard treatments. TMS is gaining ground in veteran mental health care, now available at a growing number of VA medical centers and veteran-focused clinics.

## Why TMS for veterans

- **Treatment-resistant depression:** Many veterans have been through multiple medications without real relief
- **PTSD comorbidity:** Depression and PTSD often travel together. TMS can address both.
- **Medication burden:** When you're already on several medications, TMS adds no drug interactions
- **TBI-related depression:** Post-TBI depression often responds poorly to medication but well to TMS
- **No sedation:** You can drive, work, and take care of your family after every session

## VA TMS programs

The VA has actually been ahead of the curve on TMS:
- 20+ VA medical centers now offer it
- VA research has contributed significantly to TMS evidence, especially for PTSD
- If you're enrolled in VA healthcare, you can access TMS through internal referral
- Community Care referrals are available if your local VA doesn't have TMS

## TRICARE coverage

Active duty, retirees, and dependents covered under TRICARE can access TMS:
- Prior authorization is required
- You'll need 2+ documented medication failures
- Available at both military treatment facilities and civilian network providers

## TMS for veteran PTSD

TMS is FDA-cleared for depression, not PTSD — but the research in veteran populations is promising:
- VA-sponsored trials targeting right DLPFC for PTSD symptoms
- Deep TMS studies for combat-related PTSD
- Some VA centers offer TMS for PTSD under research protocols
- Many veterans with PTSD also qualify for TMS under the depression indication — because the two almost always overlap

## Finding veteran-focused care

- **VA medical centers:** Ask your VA psychiatrist about TMS availability
- **Veteran-focused clinics:** Some private TMS practices specialize in veteran care
- **Clinical trials:** The VA actively recruits for TMS studies (search ClinicalTrials.gov for "TMS veterans")
- **Non-profits:** Organizations like the Steven A. Cohen Military Family Clinics offer mental health services including referrals for TMS

## What to Expect at Your First Visit

Your initial consultation typically includes a psychiatric evaluation, review of your treatment history, discussion of TMS candidacy, and insurance verification. Most clinics offer free consultations. The clinic will handle prior authorization if you move forward with treatment.
