## Mental health in the LGBTQ+ community

LGBTQ+ individuals experience depression at 2-3x the rate of the general population, driven by minority stress, discrimination, social rejection, and internalized stigma. Despite higher need, many LGBTQ+ people face barriers to mental health care — including providers who lack cultural competence.

## Why LGBTQ+ affirming care matters for TMS

TMS treatment involves 36 sessions over 6-9 weeks — a significant therapeutic relationship with your provider. Working with an affirming team matters:

- **Trust:** You need to feel safe disclosing your full mental health picture, including stressors related to identity
- **Assessment accuracy:** Providers need to distinguish between depression caused by minority stress vs other factors to set appropriate treatment expectations
- **Holistic care:** Gender-affirming hormone therapy, HIV treatment, and other medications don't interact with TMS — but your provider should know your full medical picture without judgment
- **Continuity:** If you need follow-up or maintenance, a welcoming environment encourages ongoing care

## What to look for in a provider

- Clinic explicitly states LGBTQ+ welcome/affirming on their website or materials
- Intake forms include inclusive gender identity and pronoun options
- Staff use chosen name and pronouns consistently
- Provider understands minority stress model and its impact on depression
- Experience treating LGBTQ+ patients with TMS specifically
- No assumptions about your identity, relationships, or gender

## TMS-specific considerations

- **Hormone therapy:** Gender-affirming hormones (estrogen, testosterone) do not interact with TMS or affect treatment parameters
- **Gender dysphoria:** While TMS treats depression, it does not directly address gender dysphoria — but treating comorbid depression can improve overall wellbeing
- **HIV/AIDS:** Antiretroviral medications have no interaction with TMS; depression in people living with HIV responds well to TMS
- **Trauma history:** LGBTQ+ individuals may have trauma from discrimination; ensure your provider can address this in overall treatment planning

## Finding LGBTQ+ friendly TMS clinics

- Search TMS List for clinics that indicate LGBTQ+ affirming care
- Check clinic websites for diversity statements and inclusive language
- Ask the clinic directly: "Do you have experience treating LGBTQ+ patients?"
- LGBTQ+ community health centers may offer TMS or referrals
- National Queer and Trans Therapists of Color Network and similar directories
