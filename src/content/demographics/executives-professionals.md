## Depression treatment that fits your schedule

High-performing professionals face unique barriers to depression treatment: confidentiality concerns, demanding schedules, and reluctance to try medications that might affect cognitive sharpness. TMS addresses all three.

## Why executives choose TMS

- **No cognitive side effects:** Unlike antidepressants that can cause brain fog, fatigue, or emotional blunting — TMS preserves (and may enhance) mental acuity
- **Confidential:** No pharmacy records, no prescription history, no medication side effects visible to colleagues
- **Time-efficient:** Theta burst sessions take 3 minutes. Even standard sessions (19-37 minutes) can fit into a lunch break
- **No ongoing medication:** Complete a 6-9 week course, then done — no daily pills
- **Performance-compatible:** Return to work immediately after each session

## Scheduling options

**Standard schedule:**
- Daily sessions before work (7-8am slots available at many clinics)
- Lunchtime sessions (19-minute theta burst)
- After-work sessions

**Accelerated for travelers:**
- 2-3 sessions per day over 2-3 weeks
- SAINT protocol: 5 days of intensive treatment
- Ideal for executives who can block one week

## Executive TMS programs

Some TMS clinics offer specialized executive programs:
- **Private treatment suites** — not shared waiting rooms
- **Concierge scheduling** — flexible, priority booking
- **Integrated care** — TMS + executive coaching or therapy
- **Outcome tracking** — cognitive and performance metrics alongside mood
- **Travel programs** — intensive courses for out-of-town executives

## Confidentiality

- TMS clinics are HIPAA-compliant — your treatment is private
- No prescription records in pharmacy databases
- Insurance claims are coded as outpatient psychiatric — same as any therapy visit
- For maximum privacy: some executives self-pay to avoid insurance records entirely

## ROI of treatment

Untreated depression costs professionals significantly:
- Estimated productivity loss of $10,000-$44,000/year per depressed employee (WHO data)
- TMS at $8,000-$12,000 is a one-time investment with 6-12 month benefit
- Many executives report improved decision-making, energy, and leadership effectiveness post-treatment
