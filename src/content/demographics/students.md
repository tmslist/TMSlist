## Student depression is at crisis levels

College student depression rates have tripled since 2013. Campus counseling centers are overwhelmed with months-long wait lists. Many students try antidepressants that cause side effects — weight gain, sexual dysfunction, cognitive dulling — that interfere with academic performance. TMS offers a different approach.

## Why students consider TMS

- **No medication side effects:** Antidepressant side effects (fatigue, brain fog) directly impact studying and test performance. TMS has no cognitive side effects.
- **Time-limited:** 6-9 weeks of treatment, then done — vs taking pills indefinitely
- **Compatible with academics:** 3-minute theta burst sessions between classes
- **No interaction with anything:** Safe with caffeine, alcohol (not recommended, but realistic for college), birth control, and other common student medications

## Scheduling around classes

- **Between classes:** Theta burst (3 min) + travel time = doable between back-to-back classes if clinic is near campus
- **Morning before class:** Many clinics offer 7-8am slots
- **Summer/break treatment:** Start during summer break to avoid class conflicts
- **Reduced course load:** Some students temporarily reduce credits during TMS treatment

## Insurance for students

**If on parent's insurance (most common under 26):**
- Most commercial plans cover TMS after medication failures
- Parent's plan handles authorization — student may need to coordinate
- Network may be limited if attending school in a different state from parents

**Student health insurance:**
- Coverage varies significantly by university
- Some university plans explicitly cover TMS; others don't
- Contact student health center for guidance

**No insurance:**
- Some TMS clinics offer student discounts or payment plans
- Clinical trials at university hospitals provide free treatment
- University counseling centers may know of local resources

## Campus resources

- **University counseling center:** Can document medication failures and refer to TMS
- **Student disability services:** Depression is a recognized disability; accommodations during treatment may be available
- **Health center psychiatry:** May have TMS knowledge or be able to refer
- **Research participation:** University psychology/psychiatry departments often run TMS studies

## Academic accommodations during TMS

- Request scheduling flexibility from professors (daily appointments for 6-9 weeks)
- Student disability services can provide official accommodation letters
- Some students qualify for reduced course load or incomplete grades during treatment
- TMS itself doesn't impair academic ability — but the depression being treated does

## What parents should know

- TMS is FDA-approved and safe for adults 18+
- No sedation — your student can attend class, study, and drive normally after sessions
- The biggest challenge is the daily schedule commitment
- Many students report improved academic performance within weeks of starting TMS
- Help coordinate insurance authorization early — the process takes 1-2 weeks
