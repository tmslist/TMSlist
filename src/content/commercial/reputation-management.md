## Your Online Reputation Drives Patient Decisions

TMS is unfamiliar to most people. They're already nervous about trying something new. Seeing that others had a good experience at your clinic? That's often the difference between them calling you or calling someone else.

## Where Your Reputation Lives

### Primary Review Platforms
- **Google Business Profile** — the big one, shows right in search results
- **Healthgrades** — heavily used for finding specialists
- **Zocdoc** — matters if you accept online booking
- **Yelp** — less healthcare-focused, but people still check
- **Psychology Today** — common for psychiatry searches
- **TMS List** — where people compare TMS clinics specifically

### Secondary Platforms
- Facebook recommendations
- RealSelf (if you also offer aesthetics)
- Vitals.com
- WebMD physician directory

## How to Collect More Reviews

### The Ask
Most happy patients will leave a review. They just need a nudge.

1. **Time it right** — ask after session 20+ when they're feeling better, not on day one
2. **Make it dead simple** — hand them a card with a QR code that goes straight to your Google review page
3. **Follow up** — a friendly email or text after treatment ends with a direct review link
4. **Get your techs involved** — they've spent 6 weeks building rapport. They're in the best position to ask.

### What Not to Do
- Don't offer incentives for reviews (violates platform policies and potentially FTC guidelines)
- Don't write fake reviews or have staff pose as patients
- Don't only ask the patients you know are happy — that kind of selection bias undermines credibility
- Don't post reviews that include PHI without explicit written consent

## Responding to Negative Reviews

They happen. How you handle them matters more than the review itself.

### Do
- Respond within 24–48 hours
- Thank the reviewer for sharing their experience
- Acknowledge their concern without getting defensive
- Invite them to reach out directly to resolve things
- Keep it short and professional

### Don't
- Ignore it
- Get defensive or argue
- Reveal any patient health information (that's a HIPAA violation)
- Offer compensation publicly
- Copy-paste the same response on every review

### Template Response
> "Thank you for sharing your experience. We take all feedback seriously and want to make sure every patient has a positive experience. We'd appreciate the chance to discuss your concerns directly — please contact our office at [phone] at your convenience."

## Monitoring Your Reputation

- **Set up Google Alerts** for your clinic name
- **Check reviews weekly** across all platforms
- **Track your average rating** monthly
- **Respond to every review** — good and bad
- **Report fake or spam reviews** to the platform
