## Your Website Is Your First Impression

Most TMS patients will check out your website before they ever pick up the phone. If it looks outdated, loads slowly, or doesn't answer their questions, they'll hit the back button and try the next clinic. Your site needs to educate, build trust, and make it easy to take the next step.

## Must-Have Pages

### Homepage
- Clear statement of what you do (TMS therapy for depression)
- Location and contact info above the fold
- "Schedule a Consultation" button — prominent, not buried
- Insurance logos
- Brief credentials (board-certified, FDA-cleared devices)

### Treatment Pages
One page for each condition you treat:
- TMS for Depression
- TMS for OCD
- TMS for Anxiety
- Each targeting local search terms

### About / Meet the Team
- Real provider photos, bios, and credentials
- Your TMS experience (how many patients you've treated)
- Certifications and hospital affiliations
- Why you chose TMS — a personal touch goes a long way

### Insurance and Cost
- List of accepted insurance carriers
- What the prior authorization process looks like
- Self-pay pricing (if you offer it)
- Financing options

### Patient Resources
- What to expect during TMS
- How to prepare for your first session
- FAQ page
- Patient testimonials and reviews

### Contact
- Phone number, address, hours
- Contact form
- Embedded Google Map
- Online scheduling link (if available)

## Design Principles for Medical Websites

1. **Clean and modern** — skip the cluttered layouts, stethoscope stock photos, and blue-and-white-everything approach
2. **Mobile-first** — 60%+ of healthcare searches happen on phones
3. **Fast loading** — under 3 seconds, especially on mobile
4. **Accessible** — WCAG 2.1 AA compliance (alt text, keyboard navigation, sufficient contrast)
5. **HIPAA-aware** — contact forms should be secure, and don't collect PHI unless you need to

## What Converts Visitors Into Patients

- **Phone number in the header** — clickable on mobile
- **Clear call to action on every page** — "Schedule a Free Consultation"
- **Insurance verification tool** — even a simple "Do you accept my insurance?" form
- **Patient reviews** — embedded Google Reviews or testimonials
- **Video** — a 2-minute "What is TMS?" video on the homepage boosts engagement significantly

## Common Mistakes

- **No mobile optimization** — instant credibility killer
- **No calls to action** — beautiful info with no way to act on it
- **Stock photos only** — real photos of your facility and team build far more trust
- **Buried contact info** — if people can't find your phone number in 5 seconds, they leave
- **No SEO** — a gorgeous website nobody finds is worthless
