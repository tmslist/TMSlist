## When You Need a TMS Consultant

Adding TMS to your practice — or launching a standalone clinic — means making a stack of decisions that directly affect whether you make money or lose it. A good TMS consultant helps you skip the expensive mistakes and get to profitability faster.

## What TMS Consultants Help With

### Business Planning
- Market analysis — is there enough demand in your area to support a TMS practice?
- Competitive landscape — who else offers TMS nearby, and how are they positioned?
- Financial projections — revenue modeling based on realistic patient volume and payer mix
- Break-even analysis — when does your TMS investment pay for itself?

### Device Selection
- Comparing FDA-cleared devices (NeuroStar, BrainsWay, MagVenture, CloudTMS, Nexstim)
- New vs. certified pre-owned equipment
- Lease vs. purchase analysis
- Manufacturer contract negotiation
- Maintenance and service agreement review

### Clinical Protocol Development
- Standard vs. theta burst vs. deep TMS protocol selection
- Motor threshold mapping procedures
- Treatment parameter guidelines
- Outcome measurement systems (PHQ-9 tracking, response/remission rates)
- Safety protocols (seizure management, contraindication screening)

### Operational Setup
- Treatment room design and equipment layout
- Patient flow optimization (scheduling, intake, treatment, checkout)
- Staffing models (how many technicians per device, supervision structure)
- EHR configuration for TMS documentation
- Prior authorization workflow design

### Insurance and Revenue
- Credentialing strategy (which carriers to go after first)
- Rate negotiation guidance
- Billing workflow optimization
- Denial management protocols
- Revenue cycle benchmarking

## When to Hire a Consultant

- **Before buying a device** — this is the biggest financial decision, so get informed analysis first
- **When patient volume stalls** — not hitting 6–8 patients per day within 6 months? Something's off.
- **When denial rates are high** — above 15% points to a process problem
- **When expanding** — adding a second device or opening another location
- **When outcomes disappoint** — a protocol review might be what you need

## What to Expect

A typical engagement looks like this:

| Phase | Duration | Deliverables |
|---|---|---|
| Assessment | 1–2 weeks | Market analysis, practice review, gap identification |
| Planning | 2–4 weeks | Business plan, financial model, implementation timeline |
| Implementation support | 2–3 months | Vendor coordination, credentialing, staff training |
| Optimization | Ongoing | Monthly reviews, KPI tracking, operational adjustments |

## ROI of Consulting

A TMS device costs $70,000–$150,000. Credentialing takes 3–6 months. Marketing takes time to ramp. The gap between a well-planned launch (breakeven in 6–9 months) and a poorly planned one (breakeven in 18+ months — or never) often comes down to $100,000+ in lost revenue.

Good consulting pays for itself several times over by compressing the path to profitability.
