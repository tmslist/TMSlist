## Marketing a TMS Practice Is Different

This isn't like marketing a general psychiatry practice. Most people don't know what TMS is. Half your referral sources don't fully get it either. And your competitors range from hospital systems to private practices to slick direct-to-consumer brands. Cookie-cutter healthcare marketing won't cut it here.

## What TMS Clinics Actually Need

### Patient Education
Most people searching for TMS are still researching — not ready to book. Your marketing has to teach first:
- What TMS is and how it works
- Whether insurance covers it
- What treatment actually feels like
- How to tell if they're a candidate

### Referral Network Development
The majority of TMS patients still come through referrals:
- Primary care physicians
- Psychiatrists who don't offer TMS
- Therapists and psychologists
- Employee assistance programs (EAPs)

Building these relationships takes targeted outreach, education, and regular follow-up. One lunch-and-learn isn't enough.

### Digital Presence
Your website needs to:
- Rank for local TMS searches
- Turn visitors into consultation requests
- Offer insurance verification tools
- Show real patient outcomes and reviews

## Common Marketing Mistakes

1. **Treating TMS like a commodity** — people want expertise and trust, not the cheapest option
2. **Ignoring SEO** — "TMS therapy near me" and "TMS for depression [city]" are high-value searches you're giving away
3. **No patient reviews** — social proof matters even more for a treatment most people haven't heard of
4. **Forgetting referring providers** — spending everything on direct-to-patient marketing misses half your pipeline
5. **Generic messaging** — "we care about you" doesn't set your TMS practice apart from anyone

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do TMS clinics typically attract new patients?
The most effective channels for TMS clinics are: (1) psychiatrist and primary care physician referrals — build relationships with local prescribers who treat depression but don't offer TMS; (2) insurance-authorized patient pipelines — most TMS patients come through insurance, so being in-network with major carriers is critical; (3) organic search — patients searching "TMS therapy near me" and "[city] TMS clinic" are high-intent; (4) patient education content that builds trust before they're ready to call.

### What's the biggest marketing mistake TMS clinics make?
Most TMS clinics market like a general healthcare practice. They don't account for the fact that most patients have never heard of TMS. You need to educate before you can convert. Content that explains what TMS is, how it works, and why it's different from medication or therapy — combined with clear insurance information — converts far better than generic "schedule an appointment" CTAs.

### Should TMS clinics invest in Google Ads or SEO?
Both — but start with SEO and Google Business Profile optimization. These are free, high-ROI, and build long-term compounding traffic. Google Ads for "TMS therapy" can cost $15–$40 per click depending on your market, so SEO reduces your cost-per-acquisition significantly over time. Use paid search to fill gaps while organic rankings build.
