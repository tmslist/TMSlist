## Before your first appointment

**1. Eat a normal meal**

Don't show up on an empty stomach. TMS doesn't require fasting — you're not being sedated. Having food in your system helps prevent lightheadedness.

**2. Take your medications as usual**

Unless your psychiatrist specifically tells you to stop or adjust something, keep taking your current medications. Most people continue their antidepressants during TMS treatment.

**3. Skip the caffeine excess**

Your normal coffee is fine. But don't double up. Caffeine can increase scalp sensitivity and make the tapping sensation more noticeable during your first session.

**4. Remove metal and electronics**

You'll be near a powerful magnet. Remove earrings, hair clips, piercings near your head, and any metal jewelry above the neck. Leave your phone and credit cards in the waiting room — they won't be damaged, but the clinic will ask you to store them away from the treatment chair.

**5. Bring entertainment**

Sessions last 19-37 minutes. Most clinics let you watch TV, listen to music (not through earbuds — you'll wear earplugs), or just relax. Bring a show queued up on your phone to watch on the clinic's screen.

## During the first session (mapping)

Your first visit takes longer — usually 45-60 minutes instead of the normal 20. That's because the technician needs to "map" your brain to find the right treatment spot.

**What mapping involves:**
- The coil is placed on your head and moved around while delivering single pulses
- They're looking for the spot that makes your thumb twitch (the motor cortex)
- From that reference point, they calculate exactly where to aim the coil for depression treatment
- They'll also calibrate the intensity — the minimum power needed to make your thumb move (your "motor threshold")

This process isn't painful, but it feels weird. The thumb twitching is involuntary and a little unsettling the first time. Totally normal.

## What treatment actually feels like

Once mapping is done (first session only), treatment goes like this:

1. You sit in a reclining chair
2. The technician positions the coil against your head
3. The machine delivers pulses in bursts — you'll hear loud clicking and feel tapping on your scalp
4. Each burst lasts a few seconds, followed by a pause
5. This repeats for 19-37 minutes depending on the protocol

The tapping ranges from "mildly annoying" to "uncomfortable" for the first few sessions. It gets better. Most people say it's completely tolerable by session 5-6.

## After your session

- You can drive yourself home right away
- Go back to work, exercise, eat — no restrictions
- If you get a headache, take Tylenol or Advil
- Some people feel slightly tired the first few times; this is normal

## The schedule

- **Standard protocol**: 5 sessions per week for 6 weeks (36 total)
- **Accelerated protocols** (like SAINT): Multiple sessions per day over 1-2 weeks
- **Each session**: 19-37 minutes

Plan your calendar accordingly. The daily commitment is the biggest practical challenge — but sessions are short enough that many people go during lunch breaks or before work.
