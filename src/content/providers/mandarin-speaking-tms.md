## TMS in Your Language

Over 3 million Mandarin and Cantonese speakers live in the US alone. Finding mental health care in your preferred language is hard enough — and TMS requires weeks of daily visits with ongoing provider communication. Being able to do all of that in Mandarin removes a real barrier.

## Where to Find Mandarin-Speaking TMS Providers

### United States
- **California** — San Francisco (Richmond, Sunset), Los Angeles (San Gabriel Valley, Monterey Park), San Jose
- **New York** — Flushing, Chinatown (Manhattan), Brooklyn
- **Texas** — Houston, Dallas (Plano, Richardson)
- **Washington** — Seattle (Bellevue)
- **Massachusetts** — Boston
- **New Jersey** — Edison, Fort Lee

### Asia-Pacific
- **Taiwan** — TMS is widely available in Taipei and major cities
- **Hong Kong** — private psychiatric clinics offer TMS
- **Singapore** — select psychiatric practices
- **Australia** — Sydney, Melbourne (areas with Chinese communities)

## Cultural Context

Mental health stigma in Chinese communities can be significant. Here's framing that helps when talking to family:

- **TMS is a medical procedure, not talk therapy** — it targets specific brain circuits, like how physical therapy targets specific muscles
- **FDA-cleared since 2008** — this is established medicine, not experimental
- **No medication side effects** — this matters in communities where Chinese medicine traditions make people cautious about systemic medications
- **Daily sessions are brief** — 19 minutes for theta burst, easy to fit into a work schedule

## Finding Providers

1. Use our [clinic directory](/us/) and look at metro areas with Chinese communities
2. Call clinics directly and ask for Mandarin-speaking staff
3. Chinese-American mental health organizations keep provider directories
4. University medical centers in major metros often have multilingual psychiatry departments

## What to Expect at Your First Visit

Your initial consultation typically includes a psychiatric evaluation, review of your treatment history, discussion of TMS candidacy, and insurance verification. Most clinics offer free consultations. The clinic will handle prior authorization if you move forward with treatment.
