## Can You Walk In for TMS?

Not for treatment itself — TMS requires an evaluation, a treatment plan, and usually insurance authorization first. But many clinics welcome walk-ins for **consultations and evaluations**. That's how treatment starts.

## What "Walk-In" Means for TMS

### Walk-In Consultation
You show up without a prior appointment and:
- Learn about TMS and see the equipment
- Meet the treating psychiatrist or clinical team
- Talk through your treatment history and whether TMS makes sense
- Get help starting the insurance authorization process

### Walk-In Evaluation
Some clinics do same-day psychiatric evaluations for new people:
- Full clinical assessment
- PHQ-9 depression screening
- Review of your medication history
- Treatment recommendation (which may include TMS)

### Walk-In Treatment Sessions
Once you're an active TMS patient with a treatment plan in place, some clinics let you come in during operating hours without a fixed appointment time. Flexible scheduling for people with unpredictable days.

## Finding Walk-In Friendly Clinics

- **Private practices and boutique clinics** are more likely to take walk-ins than hospital systems
- **Clinics in medical plazas** tend to be more accessible and walk-in friendly
- **Multi-provider clinics** with several TMS machines have more scheduling flexibility
- **Call ahead anyway** — even "walk-in welcome" clinics appreciate a heads-up

## What You Don't Need to Walk In

- **A referral** — most TMS clinics accept self-referrals
- **A formal diagnosis** — the clinic can evaluate and diagnose you
- **Insurance pre-approval** — the consultation itself doesn't need prior auth
- **Previous medical records** — helpful, but not required for an initial visit

## What to Bring If You Walk In

- Insurance card
- List of current medications
- Names and dosages of antidepressants you've tried before
- Any medical records you have handy
- Questions you want answered
