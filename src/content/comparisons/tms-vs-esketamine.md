## Two FDA-approved options for treatment-resistant depression

Both TMS and Spravato (esketamine nasal spray) are FDA-approved specifically for treatment-resistant depression (TRD). They represent different approaches — brain stimulation vs rapid-acting pharmacology — and have distinct practical profiles.

### Mechanism of action

**TMS:** Magnetic pulses stimulate the left DLPFC, gradually restoring normal activity in mood circuits over weeks of treatment. Effects build cumulatively through neuroplastic changes.

**Spravato:** Esketamine blocks NMDA glutamate receptors, triggering rapid synaptic plasticity and new neural connections. Derived from ketamine, it works through an entirely different pathway than traditional antidepressants.

### Efficacy comparison

| Outcome | TMS | Spravato |
|---------|-----|---------|
| Response rate | 50-60% | 50-70% |
| Remission rate | 30-35% | 25-35% |
| Speed of response | 2-4 weeks | Days to 1-2 weeks |
| Maintenance needed | Maybe (6-12 months) | Yes (ongoing) |

### Treatment experience

**TMS sessions:**
- 36 sessions over 6-9 weeks (weekdays)
- 19-37 minutes per session
- Sit in a chair, no sedation
- Drive yourself to/from clinic
- Can return to work immediately after

**Spravato sessions:**
- Twice weekly for 4 weeks, then weekly, then every 1-2 weeks indefinitely
- Self-administer nasal spray at a certified clinic
- 2-hour mandatory monitoring period after each dose
- Cannot drive for rest of the day
- Must take with an oral antidepressant (Spravato is always add-on therapy)

### Side effects

**TMS:** Scalp discomfort, mild headache. No systemic effects. No sedation. No driving restrictions.

**Spravato:** Dissociation (feeling detached from reality), dizziness, nausea, sedation, increased blood pressure, anxiety. Effects typically resolve within 2 hours. Must be monitored at a certified REMS clinic.

### Insurance and cost

**TMS:**
- Widely covered after 2+ medication failures
- $200-$400/session; $6,000-$12,000 total course
- Treatment ends after 6-9 weeks (may need retreatment in 6-12 months)

**Spravato:**
- FDA-approved — covered by most insurers with prior auth
- $600-$900 per session before insurance
- Ongoing indefinitely — total annual cost can be substantial
- Must always be paired with an oral antidepressant

### Key decision factors

**Choose TMS if:**
- You want a time-limited treatment (done in 6-9 weeks)
- You need to drive and work on treatment days
- You want to avoid sedation and dissociative effects
- You prefer not to take ongoing medication

**Choose Spravato if:**
- You need the fastest possible response (days vs weeks)
- You're comfortable with ongoing maintenance sessions
- You can handle the 2-hour monitoring requirement
- You're already on an antidepressant you want to keep

### Can you try both?

Yes. Some patients try TMS first and add or switch to Spravato if response is insufficient. Others start with Spravato for rapid relief and then do TMS for longer-term maintenance. Discuss sequencing with your psychiatrist based on your symptom severity and practical constraints.
