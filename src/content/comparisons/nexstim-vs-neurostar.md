## MRI-guided precision vs standard targeting

Nexstim uses your brain MRI to target TMS pulses with millimeter accuracy. NeuroStar uses the standard 5cm rule and motor threshold-based positioning. The big question: does more precise targeting actually mean better results?

### Technology

**Nexstim SmartFocus TMS:**
- MRI-guided neuronavigation system
- Uses your brain MRI to create a 3D map
- Real-time tracking of coil position relative to target
- E-field modeling shows exactly where stimulation reaches
- Originally developed for presurgical brain mapping — so the precision pedigree is real

**NeuroStar Advanced Therapy:**
- Standard figure-8 coil with contact sensing
- Motor threshold-based targeting (5cm anterior from motor hotspot)
- No neuroimaging integration
- Automated treatment delivery
- Most widely used system

### Targeting precision

**Nexstim:** Targets your specific DLPFC based on your actual brain anatomy. Accounts for individual variation in brain folding, cortical thickness, and target location. Precision: ~2-3mm.

**NeuroStar:** Uses a population-average rule — 5cm forward from your motor cortex. Works for most people, but studies show the actual DLPFC location varies by 1-3cm across individuals. That means the standard approach might miss the sweet spot for some.

### Does precision matter?

The research says: probably.
- A 2022 Stanford study found that targeting the specific DLPFC subregion most connected to the subgenual cingulate (a depression hub) predicted better outcomes
- Some studies show higher response rates with MRI-guided targeting (65-70%) vs standard (50-60%)
- But — and this is important — no large randomized head-to-head trial has been completed yet

So the signal is promising. The proof isn't airtight.

### Who should consider Nexstim

- You've tried standard TMS and it didn't work — precision targeting might hit a spot the standard approach missed
- You're willing to get a brain MRI for personalized targeting
- You're at an academic medical center or precision psychiatry program that offers it
- You have atypical brain anatomy

### The bottom line

Nexstim offers the most precise TMS targeting you can get right now. Whether that precision consistently leads to better outcomes for the average person isn't proven by large trials yet. But if you've tried standard TMS without success, Nexstim's personalized approach is worth looking into. Sometimes the difference between "didn't work" and "worked" is a few millimeters.
