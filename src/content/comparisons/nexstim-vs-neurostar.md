## MRI-guided precision vs standard targeting

Nexstim uses MRI-guided neuronavigation to target TMS pulses with millimeter accuracy. NeuroStar uses the standard 5cm rule and motor threshold-based positioning. The question: does more precise targeting translate to better outcomes?

### Technology

**Nexstim SmartFocus TMS:**
- MRI-guided neuronavigation system
- Uses patient's brain MRI to create 3D map
- Real-time tracking of coil position relative to target
- E-field modeling shows exactly where stimulation reaches
- Originally developed for presurgical brain mapping

**NeuroStar Advanced Therapy:**
- Standard figure-8 coil with contact sensing
- Motor threshold-based targeting (5cm anterior from motor hotspot)
- No neuroimaging integration
- Automated treatment delivery
- Most widely used system

### Targeting precision

**Nexstim:** Targets the individual patient's DLPFC based on their MRI anatomy. Can account for individual variation in brain folding, cortical thickness, and target location. Precision: ~2-3mm.

**NeuroStar:** Uses a population-average rule (5cm anterior from motor cortex). This works for most patients but may miss the optimal target in some — studies show the actual DLPFC location varies by 1-3cm across individuals.

### Does precision matter?

Research suggests it might:
- A 2022 Stanford study found that targeting the specific DLPFC subregion most connected to the subgenual cingulate (a depression hub) predicted better outcomes
- Some studies show higher response rates with MRI-guided targeting (65-70%) vs standard (50-60%)
- However, no large randomized head-to-head trial has been completed

### Practical comparison

| Factor | Nexstim | NeuroStar |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Requires MRI? | Yes (before treatment) | No |
| Setup time | Longer (initial MRI processing) | Standard |
| Session time | Similar (20-40 min) | 19-37 min |
| Availability | Limited (fewer clinics) | Widely available |
| Cost to patient | Similar | Similar |
| Insurance coverage | Same (covered as TMS) | Same |

### Who should consider Nexstim

- Patients who haven't responded to standard TMS targeting
- Those willing to get a brain MRI for personalized targeting
- Academic medical centers or precision psychiatry programs
- Patients with atypical brain anatomy

### The bottom line

Nexstim offers the most precise TMS targeting available. Whether that precision consistently translates to better outcomes for the average patient isn't yet proven by large trials. For patients who've failed standard TMS, Nexstim's personalized approach may offer an advantage worth exploring.
