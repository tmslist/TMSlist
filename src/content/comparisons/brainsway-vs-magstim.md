BrainsWay and Magstim represent two different philosophies in TMS system design. BrainsWay built its identity around the H-coil and a specific OCD indication that no other manufacturer holds. Magstim built the Rapid² as a flexible platform used extensively in research settings and clinical practice globally. Comparing them means deciding what you're treating and what's available near you.

## What You'll Learn

- How the H-coil and figure-8 coil designs differ in practice
- OCD treatment advantages for BrainsWay
- Protocol support on each platform
- US availability and insurance considerations

## Coil Design: The Core Difference

BrainsWay uses the proprietary H-coil design that targets deeper brain structures without proportionally increasing surface stimulation. This is most clinically relevant for OCD, where the anterior cingulate — a deeper structure — is a primary treatment target. The H-coil can reach it more directly than figure-8 coils.

Magstim's Rapid² uses a conventional figure-8 coil design alongside support for double-cone and other coil configurations. For depression treatment targeting the dorsolateral prefrontal cortex, the figure-8 coil is well-suited and has decades of evidence behind it.

The depth advantage only matters if your condition benefits from it. For pure depression treatment, both approaches work. For OCD, BrainsWay has a structural advantage.

## OCD: BrainsWay's Clear Edge

BrainsWay holds the only dedicated FDA clearance for Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder in TMS. That clearance came from specific multicenter trials targeting OCD patients with the deep TMS H-coil protocol.

Magstim can treat OCD off-label and some research centers do, but the insurance pathway is more straightforward with BrainsWay's labeled indication. If OCD is in your clinical picture, this matters practically.

## US Availability

BrainsWay has built a significant US commercial footprint through its OCD indication, becoming a common system in private TMS and psychiatric clinics that treat a range of conditions.

Magstim is more common in academic and research settings in the US. The Rapid² is a research favorite — it appears in a large proportion of published TMS studies — but commercial US availability is more limited than BrainsWay.

## The Bottom Line

For OCD patients: BrainsWay, clearly.

For depression-only: Both are capable. Availability and insurance relationships favor BrainsWay in most US markets. Magstim is a strong option at academic centers.
