## Two leading figure-8 coil TMS systems

NeuroStar and MagVenture are both figure-8 coil rTMS systems — meaning they use similar focused stimulation technology. Unlike BrainsWay's deep TMS (H-coil), these systems target a more precise cortical area. But they differ in features, protocols, and market positioning.

### Technology

**NeuroStar Advanced Therapy:**
- Proprietary iron-core coil design
- Built-in contact sensing ensures consistent coil placement
- Integrated SenStar treatment system with patient comfort features
- TrakStar data management system
- Touch screen interface

**MagVenture TMS Therapy:**
- Cool coil technology (active cooling reduces heat buildup)
- Flexible coil options (multiple coil shapes available)
- MagPro stimulator platform (used in both clinical and research settings)
- Can be configured for different protocols and research applications
- Popular in academic/research settings

### FDA clearances

| Indication | NeuroStar | MagVenture |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| Depression (MDD) | 2008 (first cleared) | 2015 |
| Anxious depression | 2021 | No |
| OCD | No | No |

NeuroStar has more FDA clearances and a longer commercial track record. MagVenture is widely used in research and cleared for depression.

### Session experience

**NeuroStar:**
- 19 minutes with Express TMS (theta burst) protocol
- 37 minutes with standard protocol
- Automated motor threshold determination
- Built-in comfort management

**MagVenture:**
- Session duration varies by protocol (typically 20-40 minutes)
- Manual and automated threshold options
- Cool coil reduces discomfort from heat buildup
- More protocol flexibility for clinicians

### Clinical outcomes

Both systems deliver similar efficacy when used with equivalent protocols:
- Response rates: 50-60% for both
- Remission rates: 30-35% for both
- The coil technology is similar enough that outcomes depend more on protocol parameters and provider expertise than the specific device

### Cost to patients

Virtually identical. Insurance doesn't differentiate between devices:
- $200-$400 per session
- $6,000-$12,000 per treatment course
- Coverage based on diagnosis and medication history, not device brand

### How to choose

In practice, you usually don't choose the device — you choose the clinic. The provider's expertise, location, insurance acceptance, and availability matter more than which specific system they use.

If you do have a choice:
- **NeuroStar** for the most established commercial system with the shortest session time (19 min theta burst)
- **MagVenture** if the clinic uses it for specific protocol advantages or if you want a provider with research expertise
