## Two leading figure-8 coil systems

These two use the same core technology — figure-8 coils that focus stimulation on a precise cortical area. Unlike BrainsWay's deep TMS (H-coil), they're both doing the same type of stimulation. But they differ in features, protocols, and who they're built for.

### Technology

**NeuroStar Advanced Therapy:**
- Proprietary iron-core coil design
- Built-in contact sensing ensures consistent coil placement
- Integrated SenStar treatment system with patient comfort features
- TrakStar data management system
- Touch screen interface

**MagVenture TMS Therapy:**
- Cool coil technology (active cooling reduces heat buildup)
- Flexible coil options — multiple shapes available
- MagPro stimulator platform (used in both clinical and research settings)
- Can be configured for different protocols and research applications
- A favorite in academic and research settings

### FDA clearances

| Indication | NeuroStar | MagVenture |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| Depression (MDD) | 2008 (first cleared) | 2015 |
| Anxious depression | 2021 | No |
| OCD | No | No |

NeuroStar got there first and has more clearances. MagVenture is widely used in research and cleared for depression treatment.

### Session experience

**NeuroStar:**
- 19 minutes with Express TMS (theta burst) protocol
- 37 minutes with standard protocol
- Automated motor threshold determination
- Built-in comfort management

**MagVenture:**
- Session length varies by protocol (20-40 minutes is typical)
- Manual and automated threshold options
- Cool coil reduces discomfort from heat buildup
- More protocol flexibility for clinicians who want to fine-tune things

### Clinical outcomes

Here's the thing: when you use the same protocol, both systems deliver similar results.
- Response rates: 50-60% for both
- Remission rates: 30-35% for both
- Outcomes depend more on protocol parameters and provider expertise than the box with the logo on it

### Cost to you

Virtually identical. Insurance doesn't differentiate:
- $200-$400 per session
- $6,000-$12,000 per treatment course
- Coverage is based on your diagnosis and medication history, not the device brand

### How to choose

Let's be real — you usually don't choose the device. You choose the clinic. The provider's skill, location, insurance acceptance, and appointment availability matter more than which machine is in the room.

If you do have a choice:
- **NeuroStar** for the most established commercial system with the shortest session time (19 min theta burst)
- **MagVenture** if the clinic uses it for specific protocol advantages, or if you want a provider with research-level expertise
