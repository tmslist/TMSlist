## 3 minutes vs 37 minutes. Same results.

That's not a typo. Theta burst stimulation (TBS) delivers TMS treatment in a fraction of the time. Standard rTMS takes 37 minutes per session. Intermittent TBS (iTBS) takes just 3 minutes — same number of total pulses, condensed into a rapid burst pattern.

### How they differ

**Standard rTMS:**
- 10Hz repetitive pulses
- 3,000 pulses per session
- 37 minutes per session
- 36 sessions over 6-9 weeks
- The original FDA-cleared protocol (2008)

**Intermittent Theta Burst (iTBS):**
- Bursts of 3 pulses at 50Hz, repeated at 5Hz intervals
- 600 pulses per session (fewer but more potent bursts)
- 3 minutes per session
- 36 sessions over 6-9 weeks
- FDA-cleared (NeuroStar Express) based on the THREE-D trial

### The THREE-D trial

This is the study that changed everything. Published in The Lancet in 2018, it randomized 414 patients to iTBS vs standard 10Hz rTMS. The results:

- **iTBS was non-inferior to standard rTMS** — equivalent efficacy
- Response rate: iTBS 49% vs standard 47% (no real difference)
- Remission rate: iTBS 32% vs standard 28% (no real difference)
- iTBS took 3 minutes vs 37 minutes
- No meaningful difference in side effects

That's it. Same outcomes. A fraction of the chair time.

### Why theta burst matters

- **Time:** 3 minutes vs 37 minutes — this is a game-changer if you're fitting treatment into a workday
- **Clinic throughput:** Clinics can treat more people per day
- **Sticking with it:** Easier to commit to 3-minute appointments five days a week
- **Cost potential:** Shorter sessions could mean lower costs (though most clinics charge the same right now)

### When standard rTMS might be better

- Some people report less scalp discomfort with the longer, more gradual protocol
- If iTBS didn't work for you, trying standard rTMS gives you a different stimulation pattern
- Some clinics only have standard rTMS capability — not all devices support theta burst
- Certain research protocols still use standard parameters

### Comfort comparison

**Standard rTMS:** Steady, rhythmic tapping sensation for 37 minutes. You get used to it as the session goes on.

**iTBS:** Intense bursts with pauses between them. The bursts feel sharper, but the whole thing is over in 3 minutes. Some people find the burst pattern more intense. Others just prefer ripping off the band-aid.

### Cost

Most clinics charge the same per session regardless of protocol:
- $200-$400 per session for either
- Insurance covers both equally
- If there's a cost difference, it's small

### Which should you choose?

**Choose iTBS (theta burst) if:**
- You have a busy schedule or a long commute
- You want the most convenient option available
- Your clinic offers it (not all do)

**Choose standard rTMS if:**
- You prefer gradual, steady stimulation
- iTBS didn't work and you want to try a different pattern
- Your clinic only offers standard protocols

**The bottom line:** iTBS and standard rTMS produce the same outcomes. This choice is about convenience and comfort, not effectiveness.
