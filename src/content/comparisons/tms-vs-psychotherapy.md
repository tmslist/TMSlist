## Brain stimulation vs talk therapy

Psychotherapy (including CBT, psychodynamic therapy, and interpersonal therapy) and TMS treat depression through entirely different mechanisms. Psychotherapy changes how you think about and respond to experiences. TMS changes the neural activity patterns underlying depressed mood. Both are evidence-based, and they complement each other.

### How they work

**TMS** delivers magnetic pulses to the left DLPFC, gradually increasing activity in underactive mood circuits. The treatment is passive — you sit in a chair while the device works. Effects build biologically over 4-6 weeks.

**Psychotherapy** works through conversation and skill-building. You learn to identify distorted thinking, develop coping strategies, process difficult emotions, and change behavioral patterns that maintain depression. The work is active and ongoing.

### Efficacy for depression

| Measure | TMS | Psychotherapy |
|---------|-----|--------------|
| Response rate | 50-60% | 40-60% (varies by type) |
| Remission rate | 30-35% | 30-40% |
| Time to effect | 2-4 weeks | 8-16 weeks |
| Relapse prevention | Moderate (may need retreatment) | Strong (skills persist) |
| Best evidence for | Treatment-resistant depression | Mild-moderate depression, prevention |

### Practical comparison

**TMS:**
- 36 sessions over 6-9 weeks, then done
- No homework or active effort required during sessions
- Requires a certified TMS clinic
- $6,000-$12,000 (insurance covers after med failures)

**Psychotherapy:**
- Weekly sessions for months to years
- Requires active engagement and homework
- Available from thousands of therapists, including via telehealth
- $150-$250/session, most insurance covers

### The case for combining both

Research increasingly supports combining TMS and psychotherapy:

- TMS may restore the brain's capacity to learn, making therapy more effective
- Therapy provides the coping skills that prevent relapse after TMS ends
- Some clinics time therapy sessions immediately after TMS for maximum neuroplastic benefit
- A 2024 study found combined TMS + CBT had higher remission rates than either alone

### Which first?

- **Psychotherapy first** if your depression is mild-moderate and you haven't tried structured therapy
- **TMS first** if depression is severe enough that you can't engage in therapy effectively
- **Both together** if you want the best chance of lasting recovery and can manage the schedule
