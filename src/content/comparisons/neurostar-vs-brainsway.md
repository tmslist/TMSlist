## The two biggest names in TMS

NeuroStar and BrainsWay dominate the US TMS market, but they use fundamentally different technology. NeuroStar uses a focused figure-8 coil for repetitive TMS (rTMS), while BrainsWay uses a patented H-coil for deep TMS (dTMS) that reaches broader, deeper brain structures.

### Technology

**NeuroStar Advanced Therapy:**
- Figure-8 coil design — focuses magnetic pulses on a small, precise area (~2cm depth)
- Targets the left DLPFC specifically
- Standard protocol: 19 minutes per session (with theta burst)
- Most widely installed TMS system in the US

**BrainsWay Deep TMS:**
- H-coil design — stimulates wider and deeper brain regions (~4cm depth)
- Can target multiple areas simultaneously
- Standard protocol: 20 minutes per session
- Helmet-based design (coil built into a padded helmet)

### FDA clearances

| Condition | NeuroStar | BrainsWay |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Depression (MDD) | 2008 | 2013 |
| OCD | No | 2018 |
| Smoking cessation | No | 2020 |
| Anxious depression | 2021 | No |

BrainsWay has more FDA clearances across conditions. NeuroStar has the longest track record for depression specifically.

### Efficacy for depression

Both devices have strong clinical data:

- **NeuroStar:** 83% of patients showed improvement in the real-world outcomes registry; 62% achieved response
- **BrainsWay:** 75% response rate in pivotal trial; deeper stimulation may engage reward circuits more effectively

Head-to-head studies are limited, but meta-analyses suggest comparable efficacy for depression. The choice often comes down to which conditions you're treating and provider preference.

### Session experience

**NeuroStar:**
- Sit in a reclining chair
- Small coil positioned against left forehead
- Tapping sensation at treatment site
- 19 minutes with TBS protocol, 37 minutes standard
- Can watch TV, listen to music

**BrainsWay:**
- Sit in a chair with padded helmet
- Broader sensation across forehead area
- 20 minutes per session
- Some patients find the helmet less comfortable initially

### Cost

Both systems cost roughly the same for patients:
- $200-$400 per session
- $6,000-$12,000 for full treatment course
- Insurance doesn't differentiate between devices — coverage is for "TMS" regardless

### Which should you choose?

**Consider NeuroStar if:**
- Your primary diagnosis is depression
- You want the shortest session time (19 min with TBS)
- You prefer the most widely available option (easier to find providers)

**Consider BrainsWay if:**
- You have OCD (only FDA-cleared deep TMS device for OCD)
- You want to quit smoking (FDA-cleared for cessation)
- Your psychiatrist recommends deeper stimulation
- You've tried standard rTMS without adequate response

### The bottom line

Both are effective, FDA-cleared options. Your choice of device matters less than finding an experienced provider who can optimize treatment parameters for your specific situation. Ask your TMS clinic which device they use and why — a good provider should be able to explain the rationale.
