## What's the difference?

The key distinction is depth of stimulation. Standard rTMS uses a figure-8 coil that focuses on a small cortical area at ~2cm depth. Deep TMS (dTMS) uses an H-coil inside a helmet that stimulates wider and deeper brain regions at ~4cm depth.

### Technology

**Standard rTMS (figure-8 coil):**
- Devices: NeuroStar, MagVenture, Magstim, Nexstim
- Focused stimulation of a specific cortical spot
- Primarily targets the left DLPFC for depression
- Precise but limited to superficial cortex

**Deep TMS (H-coil):**
- Device: BrainsWay (primary manufacturer)
- Broader stimulation pattern reaching deeper structures
- Can target the insula, medial PFC, and anterior cingulate cortex
- Helmet-based design with various H-coil configurations for different conditions

### FDA clearances

| Condition | Standard rTMS | Deep TMS |
|-----------|--------------|----------|
| Depression | 2008 | 2013 |
| OCD | No | 2018 |
| Smoking cessation | No | 2020 |
| Anxious depression | 2021 (NeuroStar) | No |

Deep TMS has broader condition coverage. Standard rTMS has a longer track record for depression.

### Efficacy for depression

Head-to-head comparisons are limited, but available data suggests:
- **Response rates:** Both achieve 50-60%
- **Remission rates:** Both achieve 30-35%
- Deep TMS may have slightly faster onset in some studies
- Standard rTMS has more published long-term follow-up data

For depression specifically, efficacy appears comparable.

### Where deep TMS may have an advantage

**OCD:** Deep TMS is the only FDA-cleared TMS option for OCD. The H-coil targets the anterior cingulate cortex and medial PFC — deeper structures involved in OCD circuitry that figure-8 coils can't reach effectively.

**Smoking cessation:** Deep TMS targeting the insula (a deep structure involved in addiction) received FDA clearance. Standard rTMS cannot reach the insula.

**Multiple conditions:** Patients with depression + OCD, or depression + addiction, may benefit from deep TMS's broader reach.

### Session comparison

| Factor | Standard rTMS | Deep TMS |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| Session time | 19-37 minutes | 20 minutes |
| Positioning | Coil held against head | Helmet worn |
| Comfort | Focused tapping sensation | Broader sensation |
| Sessions per course | 36 (typical) | 20-36 (varies by condition) |

### Cost

Similar for patients. Insurance covers both equally:
- $200-$400 per session
- $6,000-$12,000 per course
- Coverage depends on diagnosis, not device type

### Which should you choose?

**Standard rTMS if:**
- Your primary diagnosis is depression
- You want the shortest available sessions (19 min with NeuroStar theta burst)
- More clinic options (standard rTMS is more widely available)

**Deep TMS if:**
- You have OCD (only FDA-cleared option)
- You want to quit smoking
- You have depression that hasn't responded to standard rTMS
- Your provider recommends deeper stimulation based on your symptoms

### The clinical reality

Most patients don't choose the technology — they choose a provider. A skilled clinician with either device will likely achieve good outcomes. If you have access to both, discuss with your psychiatrist which technology better targets your specific symptoms.
