## Can you compress 6 weeks of TMS into 5 days?

Standard TMS requires 36 sessions over 6-9 weeks. Accelerated protocols aim to deliver equivalent treatment in days instead of weeks. The most notable is the Stanford SAINT protocol, which achieved remarkable results in a compressed timeframe.

### Standard vs accelerated protocols

**Standard rTMS:**
- 1 session per day, 5 days per week
- 36 sessions over 6-9 weeks
- 10Hz or theta burst stimulation
- 50-60% response, 30-35% remission

**Accelerated iTBS (compressed):**
- 2-3 sessions per day for 2-3 weeks
- Same total sessions, compressed schedule
- Uses theta burst stimulation (3-minute sessions allow multiple daily)
- Similar efficacy to standard pacing in studies

**Stanford SAINT protocol:**
- 10 sessions per day for 5 consecutive days (50 total sessions)
- MRI-guided targeting of patient-specific DLPFC subregion
- Intermittent theta burst stimulation
- 90% response rate, 79% remission rate in initial open-label study
- Randomized controlled trial confirmed significant superiority over sham

### The SAINT protocol in detail

Developed at Stanford University, SAINT (Stanford Accelerated Intelligent Neuromodulation Therapy) combines three innovations:

1. **Accelerated dosing:** 10 sessions per day, 50-minute intervals, for 5 days
2. **Precision targeting:** fMRI-guided targeting of the DLPFC subregion most connected to the subgenual cingulate (a depression hub)
3. **Higher dose:** 1,800 pulses per session (vs 600 in standard iTBS), totaling 90,000 pulses over 5 days

### Efficacy comparison

| Protocol | Response | Remission | Time | Sessions |
|----------|----------|-----------|------|----------|
| Standard rTMS (10Hz) | 50-60% | 30-35% | 6-9 weeks | 36 |
| Standard iTBS | 49% | 32% | 6-9 weeks | 36 |
| Compressed iTBS (2-3x/day) | 50-60% | 30-35% | 2-3 weeks | 36 |
| SAINT protocol | 79-90% | 52-79% | 5 days | 50 |

*SAINT numbers from clinical trials; real-world results may vary.*

### Why SAINT works better

The combination of factors — not just speed — may explain SAINT's superior outcomes:
- **Precision targeting** ensures stimulation reaches the optimal DLPFC subregion
- **Higher total dose** (90,000 pulses vs 54,000 in standard iTBS course)
- **Closely spaced sessions** may enhance neuroplastic effects (spaced repetition)
- **Patient selection** in trials may contribute to high response rates

### Availability and cost

**Standard TMS:** Widely available (1,000+ clinics). Insurance covers. $6,000-$12,000.

**Compressed iTBS:** Available at some clinics offering multiple daily sessions. Insurance may cover. Similar cost to standard.

**SAINT protocol:** Limited availability. Primarily at academic centers and specialized clinics. Often $10,000-$20,000+ (requires MRI + intensive 5-day program). Insurance coverage varies — some cover as intensive TMS, others deny.

### Who benefits from accelerated protocols

- Patients who need rapid response (not as acute as ECT-level urgency)
- Those who can't commit to 6-9 weeks of daily appointments
- Patients traveling to a TMS center from out of town
- Those who haven't responded to standard pacing

### Considerations

- Multiple daily sessions are more fatiguing — headache and scalp discomfort may be more pronounced
- SAINT requires brain MRI for targeting (additional cost and time)
- Long-term durability of accelerated protocols is still being studied
- Not all clinics have the infrastructure for intensive daily protocols
- Real-world SAINT results may not match clinical trial numbers
