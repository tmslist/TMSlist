BrainsWay and CloudTMS represent two different visions for TMS systems. BrainsWay built its reputation on the H-coil and a specific OCD indication that no other system holds. CloudTMS built a modern, cloud-native platform that's popular in European markets but still building US presence. The comparison comes down to what your clinical needs are and what's available where you live.

- How BrainsWay's deep TMS differs from CloudTMS's figure-8 coil approach
- OCD treatment advantages for BrainsWay
- Protocol capabilities on both platforms
- US availability and insurance considerations

## Technical Design: H-Coil vs Figure-8

BrainsWay's defining technology is the H-coil, a proprietary design that allows deeper brain stimulation without proportionally increasing surface stimulation. The H-coil's geometry directs the magnetic field along a different path than figure-8 coils, reaching subcortical structures like the anterior cingulate more effectively.

CloudTMS uses a conventional figure-8 coil design. The platform differentiates itself through its cloud management software rather than coil innovation. For standard prefrontal cortex targeting — which is where treatment for depression happens — the figure-8 coil is well-suited.

The depth advantage is most clinically relevant for OCD, where the anterior cingulate is a primary treatment target. For pure depression treatment, both coil designs work.

## OCD: Where BrainsWay Has a Decisive Edge

BrainsWay is the only TMS device with FDA clearance for Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder. This clearance was earned through dedicated multicenter trials specifically targeting OCD patients — something no other manufacturer has matched.

CloudTMS does not hold an OCD-specific clearance. The system can be used for OCD off-label, and some clinics do treat OCD with it, but insurance coverage for OCD is more straightforward with BrainsWay's specific indication.

If OCD is your primary or secondary diagnosis, BrainsWay has a meaningful practical advantage: clearer insurance authorization, labeled treatment protocols, and the clinical trial data to support those protocols.

## Protocol Support

**BrainsWay** supports:
- Deep TMS (dTMS) with the H-coil system
- Standard high-frequency protocols
- Theta burst stimulation (iTBS)
- OCD-specific deep TMS protocols

**CloudTMS** supports:
- Standard rTMS protocols
- Theta burst stimulation
- Paired associative stimulation

Both systems handle the evidence-backed protocols well. BrainsWay has the edge in OCD-specific protocols and deep stimulation modes.

## US Availability

BrainsWay has established a significant US commercial presence, particularly in private TMS clinics and psychiatric practices that treat OCD. The OCD indication made BrainsWay attractive for a broader patient population than traditional depression-only TMS.

CloudTMS is more common in European markets. US adoption has grown but the installed base is smaller. Finding a CloudTMS provider near you may require more specific searching.

## The Bottom Line

For OCD patients, BrainsWay is the clear choice — the only choice, really, from an indication standpoint.

For depression-only treatment, both systems are capable and comparable in clinical outcomes. The deciding factors are availability and insurance relationships, where BrainsWay has the stronger position.
