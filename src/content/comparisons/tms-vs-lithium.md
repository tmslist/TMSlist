## Two approaches to treatment-resistant depression

When first-line antidepressants fail, psychiatrists commonly consider two options: adding lithium to the current antidepressant (augmentation) or switching to TMS. Both have solid evidence for treatment-resistant depression.

### How they work

**TMS:** Magnetic stimulation of the DLPFC restores neural activity in mood circuits. Non-invasive, no systemic effects, time-limited (6-9 weeks).

**Lithium augmentation:** Adding lithium to an antidepressant enhances serotonergic signaling and promotes neuroprotective effects. One of the oldest and most studied augmentation strategies. Requires daily medication and blood monitoring indefinitely.

### Efficacy

- **TMS:** 50-60% response, 30-35% remission in medication-resistant patients
- **Lithium augmentation:** 40-50% response when added to an antidepressant, 25-30% remission
- Both are well-supported in clinical guidelines for treatment-resistant depression

### Side effects

**TMS:** Scalp discomfort, mild headache. No blood tests needed. No drug interactions.

**Lithium:** Requires regular blood level monitoring (toxic range is close to therapeutic). Side effects include:
- Weight gain
- Tremor
- Increased thirst/urination
- Thyroid dysfunction (10-20% of patients)
- Kidney function changes with long-term use
- Cognitive dulling
- Acne
- Nausea

### Monitoring requirements

| Requirement | TMS | Lithium |
|-------------|-----|---------|
| Blood tests | None | Every 3-6 months (lithium levels, thyroid, kidney) |
| Drug interactions | None | Many (NSAIDs, ACE inhibitors, diuretics) |
| Ongoing treatment | 6-9 weeks, then periodic retreatment | Daily pill indefinitely |
| Medical monitoring | None after treatment | Lifelong |

### Cost

- **TMS:** $6,000-$12,000 for course; insurance covers after medication failures
- **Lithium:** $10-$30/month for generic medication + $200-$400/year for blood monitoring

### Who should consider which

**TMS may be better if:**
- You want to avoid adding another systemic medication
- You have kidney concerns or thyroid issues
- You don't want ongoing blood monitoring
- You prefer a time-limited treatment

**Lithium may be better if:**
- You need continuous mood stabilization (especially if any bipolarity suspected)
- Cost is a primary concern
- You don't have access to a TMS clinic
- You've responded partially to your current antidepressant and want augmentation
- You have suicidal ideation (lithium has unique anti-suicidal properties)

### Important note on lithium's anti-suicide effect

Lithium is the only psychiatric medication with strong evidence for reducing suicide risk independent of its antidepressant effect. For patients with significant suicidal ideation, this unique property may make lithium the preferred choice regardless of other considerations.
