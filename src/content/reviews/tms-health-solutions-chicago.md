# TMS Health Solutions in Chicago: Built for People Who Work

**Finding TMS in Chicago** means picking between three worlds: academic medical centers with waitlists measured in months, small private practices with limited hours, and multi-location networks built around access.

TMS Health Solutions is firmly in that third camp. Three locations across **Chicagoland** — the Loop, Lincoln Park, and Oak Brook. All near public transit or with free parking.

If you need to squeeze 36 daily sessions into an actual working life, that practical stuff matters more than a fancy website.

Reliable TMS care with real scheduling flexibility. Insurance handling is above average. Clinical outcomes are solid. Not the flashiest operation — just effective.

## About the Clinic

Operating in Chicago since 2019, TMS Health Solutions grew from one Loop location to three. Every site sits near CTA stations — and anyone who's done daily medical appointments through a Chicago winter knows why that matters.

**Locations:**
- **Loop (Downtown):** Near Monroe Red/Blue Line. The go-to for downtown office workers who dash out at lunch.
- **Lincoln Park:** Close to Fullerton Brown Line. North Siders love it.
- **Oak Brook:** Suburban location with free parking. Serves the western burbs.

A board-certified psychiatrist oversees protocols across all three sites. Each location has at least two full-time TMS technicians.

### How Treatment Works

Standard model: psychiatric evaluation, motor threshold determination, and your first 3-5 sessions are supervised by the attending psychiatrist. After that, trained TMS technicians run your daily sessions, with the psychiatrist available by phone or in-person when needed.

They offer both **standard repetitive TMS** and **theta burst stimulation (TBS)**. The difference? Standard sessions run 19-37 minutes. TBS runs 3-10 minutes. Same magnetic principles, dramatically different time commitment. Your psychiatrist decides which protocol fits your clinical picture and schedule.

Three minutes versus thirty-seven. For someone catching the Brown Line back to the office, that's a game-changer.

## Technology

Different locations, different machines:

- **Loop and Lincoln Park:** NeuroStar Advanced Therapy
- **Oak Brook:** MagVenture MagPro

Both are FDA-cleared for depression. NeuroStar is the household name; MagVenture is the researchers' favorite — widely used in clinical trials with more flexibility in coil options. You stick with one location throughout your course.

## What Patients Are Saying

We analyzed about 90 reviews across Google, Healthgrades, and Yelp for all three Chicago locations.

**What keeps coming up:**
- **Scheduling flexibility is the top compliment.** Early morning slots at 7:30 AM. Late afternoon at 5:30 PM. One location even has limited Saturday availability. For a treatment demanding five days a week, this flexibility isn't a bonus — it's what makes treatment possible.
- **Insurance handled fast.** Multiple people mentioned prior authorization completed within a week. They kept you in the loop throughout. No ghosting, no "we'll get back to you."
- **CTA access.** People who ride the L genuinely appreciate not having to drive. Repeatedly.
- **Theta burst fans.** People who switched from standard to TBS loved the shorter sessions. Same results, fraction of the time.

**What could be better:**
- **Technician rotation.** A few people saw different technicians across sessions, especially when their preferred person was at another location. Continuity takes a hit.
- **Loop waiting room is tight.** Small space, peak hours, not ideal. You might end up standing.
- **OCD isn't their strength.** Depression is their wheelhouse. If you need OCD-specific treatment, they'll likely refer you elsewhere.

## FAQ

### Can I switch locations mid-treatment?
Only between the two city locations that both use NeuroStar. Moving to Oak Brook mid-course means recalibrating on a completely different device. Pick one location and stick with it.

### Does the clinic offer maintenance TMS?
Yes. After your initial 36 sessions, you can schedule maintenance sessions — usually weekly or biweekly — at any of the three locations.

### How does this compare to going to Rush or Northwestern for TMS?
Academic medical centers give you integrated psychiatric care and access to clinical trials. TMS Health Solutions gives you faster scheduling (days instead of weeks-to-months), more flexible hours, and a team that does TMS all day. For straightforward treatment-resistant depression, outcomes are comparable. The real question is how long you're willing to wait to start.
