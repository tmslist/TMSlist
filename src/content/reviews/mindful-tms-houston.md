## About the Clinic

Mindful TMS opened in 2020 with a specific mission. The founding psychiatrist, originally from Mexico City, noticed something: Houston's TMS clinics were concentrated in affluent neighborhoods, marketed to commercially insured patients. Underinsured communities and Spanish-speaking families were largely shut out.

So they built a clinic to change that.

The office is in a professional medical building near NRG Park, about 10 minutes from the Texas Medical Center. Free parking. In Houston, where everybody drives, that's not a perk — it's a necessity.

### What Sets Them Apart
- **Fully bilingual.** All front-office staff and two of three TMS technicians speak Spanish and English fluently. Not "we have a translator on call." Fluent.
- **They fight for your insurance.** A dedicated insurance specialist handles prior authorization, appeals, and benefit verification. They report spending an average of 4 hours per patient on insurance paperwork. Four hours. That's someone going to bat for you.
- **Published pricing.** Their self-pay rates are on the website. In an industry where "call for pricing" is the norm, that transparency is worth noting.
- **Medicaid acceptance.** One of the few Houston TMS clinics that takes Texas Medicaid managed care. If that's your coverage, your options just got a lot shorter — and this clinic is on the list.

## Technology

Mindful TMS runs the **MagVenture MagPro R30** with the Cool B70 figure-8 coil.

You've probably heard of NeuroStar. MagVenture is less famous with the general public but more popular in research labs — it's the device behind the majority of published TMS clinical trials. A few things stand out:

- **Cooling technology:** The Cool B70 coil runs cooler during treatment, which means less scalp discomfort. Small detail, big difference over 36 sessions.
- **Protocol flexibility:** Supports both standard 19-37 minute sessions and 3-minute theta burst protocols.
- **Research pedigree:** If the evidence base for TMS was built largely on MagVenture machines, that's a data point worth having.

Standard rTMS is the default here. Theta burst is available if you have scheduling constraints — your psychiatrist makes the final call.

## FAQ

### Is Mindful TMS a good choice if I have Medicaid?
It's one of the few Houston clinics where Medicaid is even an option for TMS. Call to verify that your specific managed care organization — Molina, Amerigroup, Superior, etc. — is accepted.

### How does the MagVenture compare to NeuroStar?
Both are FDA-cleared. Both have strong clinical evidence. Response rates are comparable. The MagVenture Cool B70 coil may be a bit more comfortable thanks to its cooling tech. Honestly, the device matters less than the skill of the person running it.

### Do they treat conditions other than depression?
Primarily treatment-resistant depression. They'll evaluate you for off-label TMS for anxiety and PTSD, but they don't currently offer the BrainsWay OCD protocol.
