## Why TMS works for depression

Depression isn't just feeling sad. In many cases, it involves underactivity in the left dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (DLPFC) — a brain region involved in mood regulation, decision-making, and motivation. TMS delivers focused magnetic pulses to this area, gradually restoring normal activity patterns.

The FDA cleared TMS for treatment-resistant depression in 2008. Since then, over a million patients have been treated in the US alone.

## Who qualifies

Most insurance companies cover TMS for depression if you meet these criteria:

- **Diagnosis**: Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)
- **Failed medications**: Tried at least 2 antidepressants from different classes at adequate dose and duration (usually 6-8 weeks each)
- **Not due to another condition**: Depression isn't caused by substance abuse, medical illness, or medication side effects

Some plans also require a history of psychotherapy (talk therapy) before approving TMS.

## What a treatment course looks like

- **Sessions**: 36 treatments over 6 weeks (5 days/week)
- **Session length**: 19-37 minutes depending on the protocol
- **Where**: Outpatient — you sit in a chair, no anesthesia
- **During treatment**: You'll feel a tapping or clicking sensation on your scalp. Most people watch TV, listen to music, or just sit quietly.
- **After**: You can drive yourself home. No recovery time needed.

## The numbers

| Measure | Rate |
|---------|------|
| Response (50%+ symptom reduction) | 50-60% |
| Remission (symptoms essentially gone) | 30-35% |
| Duration of benefit | 6-12 months typically |
| Need for maintenance sessions | 30-50% of responders |

## Side effects

TMS for depression has a remarkably clean side effect profile compared to medications:

- **Scalp discomfort** at the treatment site (most common; fades after first few sessions)
- **Mild headache** after treatment (responds to Tylenol/Advil)
- **Seizure risk**: Less than 0.1% — lower than many antidepressants

No weight gain. No sexual dysfunction. No emotional blunting. No withdrawal when treatment ends.

## Which TMS devices are used for depression

All major FDA-cleared TMS systems treat depression:

- **NeuroStar** — The most widely used system in the US. Standard figure-8 coil targeting DLPFC.
- **BrainsWay Deep TMS** — Uses an H-coil that reaches deeper brain structures. FDA-cleared for depression and OCD.
- **MagVenture** — Versatile system used in both clinical and research settings.

The device matters less than the clinic's experience with it. A well-trained technician using any of these systems will deliver good results.

## Finding a provider

When choosing a TMS clinic for depression treatment, ask:

1. **How many patients have you treated?** Look for clinics with 100+ patients under their belt.
2. **Who supervises treatment?** A board-certified psychiatrist should be involved in your care plan.
3. **What's your response rate?** Good clinics track and share their own outcome data.
4. **Do you handle insurance authorization?** Most do — and it makes the process much easier.
