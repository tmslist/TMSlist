## TMS as a treatment for chronic pain

Fibromyalgia involves widespread chronic pain, fatigue, and cognitive difficulties ("fibro fog") linked to abnormal central pain processing. The brain amplifies pain signals in fibromyalgia — which makes it a potential target for TMS.

TMS for fibromyalgia is **not FDA-approved** but has been studied in over 30 clinical trials with encouraging results.

## Which brain regions are targeted

**Primary motor cortex (M1):**
The most-studied target. High-frequency rTMS to the motor cortex can modulate descending pain inhibition pathways, reducing pain perception. Multiple randomized controlled trials support this approach.

**Dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (DLPFC):**
Targeted for the cognitive and emotional components of fibromyalgia — depression, anxiety, and catastrophizing that amplify pain.

**Some protocols target both regions** in a single session for broader symptom relief.

## What the evidence shows

- A 2023 meta-analysis of 15 RCTs found motor cortex rTMS produced a mean 30% reduction in pain scores
- Effects on pain were modest but clinically meaningful — comparable to medications like duloxetine or pregabalin
- DLPFC stimulation improved mood and fatigue more than pain itself
- Treatment effects lasted 1-3 months after completing a course
- Maintenance sessions (weekly or biweekly) can sustain benefit

## Treatment protocols used in studies

- **Motor cortex rTMS:** 10-20Hz, left M1, 2000-3000 pulses/session, 10-20 sessions
- **DLPFC rTMS:** 10Hz, left DLPFC, similar parameters to depression protocols
- **Deep TMS:** H-coil designs targeting broader networks, under investigation

## What patients report

Beyond pain reduction, patients in TMS studies commonly report:
- Improved sleep quality
- Reduced fatigue
- Better cognitive function ("less fibro fog")
- Improved mood (even in patients without diagnosed depression)

## Current limitations

- Not FDA-approved for fibromyalgia — insurance generally won't cover
- Effects are modest (30-50% pain reduction, not elimination)
- Maintenance sessions likely needed for sustained benefit
- Response varies — some patients improve significantly, others minimally
- Most studies used motor cortex protocols that differ from standard depression TMS

## Finding treatment

Because TMS for fibromyalgia is off-label, options include:
- **Clinical trials:** Search ClinicalTrials.gov for "TMS fibromyalgia"
- **Pain clinics with TMS:** Some pain management centers offer off-label TMS for chronic pain
- **Self-pay TMS clinics:** May offer off-label treatment; confirm the provider has experience with pain protocols (motor cortex targeting, not standard DLPFC)
