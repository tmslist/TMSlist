## TMS for OCD is different from TMS for depression

This is important to understand upfront: TMS for OCD targets a different brain region and uses a different device than TMS for depression.

For OCD, the FDA cleared **BrainsWay Deep TMS** in 2018, specifically using an H7 coil that targets the anterior cingulate cortex and medial prefrontal cortex — areas involved in the obsessive-compulsive circuit.

Standard TMS coils (the ones used for depression) target the surface of the brain. Deep TMS coils reach structures 3-4 cm below the surface. That depth matters for OCD.

## Who qualifies

- **Diagnosis**: Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder
- **Failed treatment**: Haven't responded adequately to SSRIs (like fluoxetine, fluvoxamine, or sertraline) and/or Cognitive Behavioral Therapy with Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP)
- **Severity**: Moderate to severe OCD (typically Y-BOCS score of 20+)

Insurance coverage for OCD-specific TMS is expanding but not yet as universal as depression coverage. Some plans cover it; others consider it investigational. Your clinic can help determine your coverage.

## What treatment involves

- **Sessions**: 29 treatments over 6 weeks
- **Session length**: About 20 minutes
- **Provocation**: This is unique to OCD treatment — before each session, you'll be briefly exposed to a personalized OCD trigger. This activates the OCD circuit so the TMS pulses can target it while it's active.
- **The provocation step**: Your clinician will work with you to identify a trigger that causes moderate (not extreme) anxiety. This is similar to exposure therapy but combined with brain stimulation.

## Outcomes

Response rates for OCD are lower than for depression, but for a condition this difficult to treat, they're meaningful:

- **Response** (30%+ reduction in Y-BOCS): 35-45%
- **Remission**: 20-25%
- **Benefit duration**: 3-6 months (maintenance sessions often needed)

## Important considerations

- **Not all TMS clinics treat OCD.** You need a clinic with BrainsWay Deep TMS (H7 coil) specifically. Standard NeuroStar or MagVenture systems are not FDA-cleared for OCD.
- **ERP therapy alongside TMS** improves outcomes. The combination is more effective than either alone.
- **Medication continuation**: Most patients continue their SSRI during TMS treatment.
