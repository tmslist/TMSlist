## VA and TMS therapy

The Department of Veterans Affairs has been at the forefront of TMS research and clinical adoption. Multiple VA medical centers now offer TMS as a treatment option for veterans with treatment-resistant depression and PTSD.

## Eligibility

VA TMS coverage is available to enrolled veterans who:

1. **Are enrolled in VA healthcare** (any priority group)
2. **Have treatment-resistant depression** documented by a VA psychiatrist
3. **Have failed adequate medication trials** (typically 2+, documented in VA medical records)
4. **Are evaluated by the VA TMS program** at a participating medical center

No separate prior authorization is needed — the VA's internal referral system handles this.

## VA medical centers with TMS programs

TMS is available at a growing number of VA facilities. Major VA TMS programs include:

- **VA Greater Los Angeles**
- **VA Palo Alto / Stanford partnership**
- **VA Providence (Butler Hospital partnership)**
- **VA Durham**
- **VA Houston**
- **VA Minneapolis**
- **VA San Diego**
- **VA Boston**

Contact your local VA medical center's mental health department to ask about TMS availability, or ask your VA psychiatrist for a referral.

## Cost to veterans

- **Service-connected disability:** No cost for TMS treatment
- **Non-service-connected, enrolled:** Copay may apply based on priority group (typically $15-$50 per visit)
- **No out-of-pocket for most:** Many veterans qualify for copay-free mental health care

## The VA TMS process

1. **Talk to your VA psychiatrist** about TMS interest
2. **Internal referral** to the VA TMS program (may require travel to a VA with TMS)
3. **TMS evaluation** by the program team
4. **Treatment course:** Standard 36-session protocol at the VA facility
5. **Follow-up:** VA mental health team monitors response

## VA research programs

The VA actively researches TMS for:
- **PTSD:** Several VA centers run TMS-for-PTSD clinical trials
- **Traumatic brain injury (TBI):** TMS for post-TBI depression and cognitive symptoms
- **Substance use disorders:** TMS for alcohol and opioid use disorders in veteran populations
- **Suicide prevention:** Accelerated TMS protocols for veterans in crisis

Ask your VA provider about active research protocols — participation may provide access to advanced protocols not yet available clinically.

## Community Care (non-VA providers)

If your VA doesn't have a TMS program, you may be eligible for Community Care:
- VA refers you to an approved non-VA TMS provider
- VA covers the cost
- Requires VA authorization before starting treatment
- Available when VA can't provide TMS within reasonable drive time or wait time

## Tips for veterans

- Start the conversation with your VA psychiatrist early — VA referrals can take weeks
- If your local VA doesn't offer TMS, ask about Community Care referral
- Document your medication history thoroughly in MyHealtheVet
- Check if your VA has active TMS research studies (may offer accelerated or advanced protocols)
- Veterans Crisis Line (988, press 1) is always available if you're in crisis while waiting for treatment
