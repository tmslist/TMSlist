## UHC TMS policy

UnitedHealthcare covers TMS under their Medical Policy for Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation. As the largest commercial health insurer in the US, their policy affects millions of members.

## Requirements

UHC requires:

1. **Diagnosis**: Major Depressive Disorder (MDD), unipolar
2. **Age**: 18 years or older (some plans may cover younger with documentation)
3. **Failed medications**: At least **2 antidepressant medications** from different classes, each at adequate dose for 6+ weeks
4. **Provider**: Treatment must be delivered by or under the supervision of a physician
5. **Device**: FDA-cleared TMS system

## Optum behavioral health

If your UHC plan manages behavioral health through **Optum**, the authorization process goes through Optum's system. This is common for employer-sponsored plans. The requirements are the same, but the phone number and submission portal may differ from standard UHC.

Check your card — if it says "Optum" or "UHC Optum" for behavioral health, contact that number for TMS authorization.

## What you'll pay

- **UHC Choice Plus / PPO (in-network)**: Typical total cost $1,000-$3,000 after deductible
- **UHC Navigate (HMO)**: Requires referral chain. Lower premiums but more paperwork
- **Oxford (UHC subsidiary)**: Same TMS policy as parent UHC

## Authorization timeline

1. Clinic submits pre-authorization with clinical documentation
2. UHC reviews within 5-15 business days (urgent requests faster)
3. Approval typically covers 30-36 sessions
4. Renewal requires updated documentation if additional sessions needed

## If denied

UHC has a structured appeal process:
- **Level 1**: Internal review by a different reviewer (30-day turnaround)
- **Level 2**: Peer-to-peer review — your psychiatrist speaks directly with a UHC medical reviewer
- **External review**: If internal appeals fail, you can request an independent external review

Document everything. Keep copies of all authorization requests, denial letters, and appeal submissions.
