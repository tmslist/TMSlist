## Does TRICARE cover TMS?

Yes. TRICARE covers TMS therapy for treatment-resistant depression. Coverage was expanded in recent years as the military health system recognized TMS as an effective option for service members, retirees, and their dependents who haven't responded to traditional treatments.

## Eligibility requirements

TRICARE covers TMS when all of the following are met:

1. **Diagnosis:** Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)
2. **Medication failures:** At least 2 adequate antidepressant trials (adequate dose for adequate duration, typically 6+ weeks each)
3. **Clinical assessment:** Documented by a psychiatrist or behavioral health provider
4. **Prior authorization:** Required before treatment begins

## Which TRICARE plans cover TMS?

- **TRICARE Prime:** Covered with referral from PCM and prior authorization
- **TRICARE Select:** Covered with prior authorization; no referral needed
- **TRICARE For Life (Medicare wrap):** Covered when Medicare approves
- **TRICARE Reserve Select:** Covered with prior authorization
- **TRICARE Young Adult:** Covered with prior authorization

## Authorization process

1. **See your behavioral health provider:** Get a documented assessment confirming treatment-resistant depression
2. **Obtain referral (Prime only):** Your primary care manager must refer you to a TMS provider
3. **Prior authorization:** Your TMS clinic submits the request to TRICARE's utilization management contractor
4. **Typical approval timeline:** 5-10 business days
5. **If denied:** You have the right to appeal through TRICARE's formal appeals process

## Cost sharing

Your out-of-pocket costs depend on your TRICARE plan:

- **Active duty:** No cost sharing
- **TRICARE Prime (retirees):** Specialist copay per visit (typically $34)
- **TRICARE Select:** Cost share after deductible (typically 20-25%)
- **Network vs out-of-network:** Higher cost share for out-of-network providers

## Finding a TRICARE-authorized TMS provider

- Use TRICARE's online provider directory to find TMS clinics that accept TRICARE
- Military treatment facilities (MTFs) at some bases offer TMS directly
- Many civilian TMS clinics accept TRICARE — call to confirm before scheduling
- Ask the clinic if they handle TRICARE prior authorization (most experienced clinics do)

## VA vs TRICARE

TRICARE and the VA are separate systems:

- **TRICARE:** For active duty, retirees, and dependents. Uses civilian network plus MTFs
- **VA:** For veterans with service-connected conditions or qualifying service. Uses VA medical centers
- Some veterans are eligible for both. Check with your VA coordinator about which system to use for TMS

## Tips for military families

- Start the referral/authorization process early — military bureaucracy adds time
- If your base has a TMS program, ask about wait times vs going off-base
- Document everything, especially if you've been treated at multiple duty stations
- TRICARE Nurse Advice Line (1-800-874-2273) can help navigate the process
- Some TMS clinics near military bases specialize in serving TRICARE patients
