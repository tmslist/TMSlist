## Does Kaiser Permanente cover TMS?

Yes. Kaiser Permanente covers TMS therapy for treatment-resistant depression across most of its regions. Because Kaiser is an integrated health system (insurance + hospitals + doctors), the TMS process works differently than with traditional insurers.

## How Kaiser's TMS process works

Kaiser operates as a closed network — you generally receive TMS at a Kaiser facility or a Kaiser-contracted provider:

1. **Start with your Kaiser psychiatrist:** You must be seeing a Kaiser behavioral health provider who documents your treatment-resistant depression
2. **Internal referral:** Your psychiatrist submits an internal referral for TMS evaluation
3. **TMS evaluation:** You meet with Kaiser's TMS team (where available) for assessment
4. **Authorization:** Kaiser reviews internally — no traditional prior auth phone calls
5. **Treatment:** Sessions are typically at a Kaiser medical center or contracted facility

## Eligibility criteria

Kaiser generally requires:

- Diagnosis of Major Depressive Disorder by a Kaiser psychiatrist
- Failure of at least 2 adequate antidepressant trials (documented in Kaiser's medical record)
- No contraindications (metal implants near treatment site, seizure history)
- Psychiatric evaluation confirming TMS candidacy

## Cost to members

- **Kaiser HMO:** Typically a specialist visit copay per session ($30-$75 depending on plan)
- **Kaiser HDHP:** Subject to deductible, then coinsurance
- **Out-of-pocket maximum:** TMS costs count toward your annual out-of-pocket max

## Regional availability

Kaiser's TMS availability varies by region:

- **Northern California:** Available at multiple medical centers
- **Southern California:** Available; growing number of locations
- **Mid-Atlantic (DC/MD/VA):** Available
- **Pacific Northwest (OR/WA):** Available at select locations
- **Colorado:** Available
- **Hawaii, Georgia:** Check with your local Kaiser facility

## Challenges with Kaiser TMS

- **Wait times:** Kaiser's integrated model can mean longer waits for TMS evaluation (4-8 weeks in some regions)
- **Limited locations:** Not all Kaiser medical centers have TMS equipment
- **Out-of-network:** Going outside Kaiser for TMS is rarely covered
- **Referral required:** You cannot self-refer — must go through Kaiser psychiatry

## Tips for Kaiser members

- Ask your psychiatrist about TMS early — don't wait until you've exhausted all medication options
- Document medication side effects and inadequate responses at every visit
- If wait times are long, ask about Kaiser's contracted external TMS providers
- Use Kaiser's member services to escalate if referral is delayed
- Check if your Kaiser region has a dedicated TMS program coordinator
