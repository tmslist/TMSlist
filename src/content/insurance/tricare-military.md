## TMS and the military mental health crisis

Depression and PTSD hit military personnel harder than the general population. That's not news. What is changing: the Department of Defense has increasingly turned to TMS for service members who haven't responded to medication -- especially when antidepressant side effects raise concerns about readiness and fitness for duty.

Active duty members, reservists, retirees, and dependents can all access TMS under Tricare. But the path to treatment looks different depending on your plan type and duty status.

## Tricare Prime vs Tricare Select for TMS

### Tricare Prime
- You need a **referral from your Primary Care Manager (PCM)** before seeing a TMS provider
- Your PCM can be at a Military Treatment Facility (MTF) or a civilian network provider
- After the referral, the TMS clinic handles prior authorization
- **Active duty cost:** $0. No copays, no cost share. Zero
- **Retirees:** Specialist copay applies (currently $34 per visit for network providers)

### Tricare Select
- **No referral needed** -- go directly to a TMS provider
- Prior authorization is still required
- Higher cost sharing than Prime: typically 20-25% coinsurance after your annual deductible
- More provider flexibility -- especially useful if you're stationed somewhere with limited MTF options

### Tricare Reserve Select (TRS)
- For drilling reservists and National Guard members not on active duty
- Covers TMS with prior authorization
- Cost sharing similar to Tricare Select
- Monthly premium required

## Covered conditions

Tricare currently covers TMS for:

- **Major Depressive Disorder (MDD):** Fully covered after 2 failed medication trials
- **Treatment-resistant depression in the context of PTSD:** Coverage is expanding. Approval depends on how well the clinical documentation makes the case

TMS for OCD under Tricare? Evaluated case by case. If you have OCD, ask your TMS clinic to submit a detailed letter of medical necessity citing the FDA clearance of BrainsWay Deep TMS for OCD.

## The referral and authorization process for active duty

Active duty service members have some unique steps:

1. **Talk to your behavioral health provider** at the MTF or through Military OneSource
2. **Get a referral from your PCM.** Non-negotiable for Tricare Prime
3. **Fitness for duty:** Your commanding officer doesn't need to approve TMS, but the behavioral health provider should document that treatment won't interfere with duty requirements
4. **Check your MTF.** Walter Reed, San Antonio Military Medical Center, and a growing number of MTFs have TMS on-site. On-site means zero cost and no network hassles
5. **No MTF option?** Your provider refers you to a civilian TMS clinic in the Tricare network. The clinic handles prior authorization
6. **Timeline:** Allow 2-3 weeks for authorization. Active duty expedited requests can sometimes process in under a week

## Out-of-network TMS with Tricare

No in-network TMS provider within reasonable distance? You have options.

- **Point-of-service (Prime):** You can see out-of-network providers, but cost sharing jumps -- 50% after a separate deductible
- **Select out-of-network:** 25-30% coinsurance plus a higher deductible
- **Network gap exception:** If Tricare's network doesn't have a TMS provider within reasonable travel distance, you can get an out-of-network provider approved at in-network rates. Call 1-800-874-2273 to start this process

That last option matters. If nobody's nearby, you shouldn't pay a penalty for it.

## VA vs Tricare: which should veterans use?

If you have both VA and Tricare eligibility, here's how they compare:

| Factor | VA | Tricare |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cost** | Usually $0 for service-connected conditions | Copays/coinsurance apply (except active duty) |
| **Wait times** | Longer -- limited VA TMS capacity | Shorter -- more civilian providers |
| **Provider choice** | VA facilities only (with some Community Care exceptions) | Any Tricare network provider |
| **Research access** | VA runs extensive TMS clinical trials | Limited |
| **PTSD focus** | VA is leading TMS-for-PTSD research | Standard depression protocol |

If PTSD is your primary concern, the VA may get you access to cutting-edge protocols you won't find in civilian settings. For speed and convenience, Tricare's civilian network is usually faster.

## Deployment and PCS considerations

- **PCS (Permanent Change of Station):** Relocating mid-treatment? Your new PCM needs to issue a new referral. The prior auth may need resubmission. Start this before your move date -- not after
- **Deployment:** TMS requires daily sessions for 6 weeks. If deployment is pending, talk to your behavioral health provider about timing. Some commands will delay non-essential orders to allow treatment completion
- **Overseas:** TMS at overseas MTFs is extremely limited. If you're stationed abroad, contact the TRICARE Overseas Program contractor about civilian provider access in your host country

## Resources for military families

- **Military OneSource:** 1-800-342-9647 -- free counseling and referral assistance, 24/7
- **TRICARE Nurse Advice Line:** 1-800-874-2273 -- help with the authorization process
- **Defense Health Agency Mental Health Portal:** Info on MTF-based TMS programs
- **Give an Hour:** Nonprofit providing free mental health services to military families, including help with TMS coverage
- **Cohen Veterans Network:** Operates clinics that may offer TMS or referrals at reduced cost for veterans

## What If You're Denied?

<div class="not-prose my-8 bg-red-50 border border-red-200 rounded-xl p-5">
  <h4 class="font-semibold text-red-800 text-sm mb-2">Don't give up after a denial</h4>
  <p class="text-sm text-red-700 mb-3">TMS denial overturn rates are <strong>60-70%</strong> on appeal. Steps to take:</p>
  <ul class="text-sm text-red-700 space-y-1 mb-0 list-disc pl-4">
    <li><strong>Request a peer-to-peer review</strong> — your psychiatrist talks directly to the insurer's medical director</li>
    <li><strong>Submit additional documentation</strong> addressing the specific denial reason</li>
    <li><strong>File a formal appeal</strong> with your state insurance department if internal appeals fail</li>
    <li><strong>External review</strong> — most states allow independent external review of coverage denials</li>
  </ul>
