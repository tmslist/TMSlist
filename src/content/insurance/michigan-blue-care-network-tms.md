## Blue Care Network of Michigan TMS Coverage

Blue Care Network (BCN), Michigan's Blue Cross Blue Shield HMO, covers TMS therapy for treatment-resistant depression under their behavioral health benefits. BCN is part of the BCBS Michigan family and follows their TMS medical policy with some plan-specific requirements.

## BCN Coverage Requirements

To get TMS approved through BCN, you'll typically need:

1. **Diagnosis:** Major Depressive Disorder confirmed by a psychiatrist
2. **Medication trials:** At least 2 failed antidepressant trials at adequate dose and duration
3. **Psychiatric evaluation:** Current assessment documenting treatment-resistant status
4. **PHQ-9 score:** Documented depression severity
5. **Prior authorization:** Required before starting treatment

## Authorization Process

1. **Referral:** Get a referral from your BCN primary care physician or psychiatrist
2. **Pre-authorization:** Your TMS clinic submits authorization to BCN
3. **Documentation:** Include medication history, PHQ-9 scores, and treatment plan
4. **Response:** BCN typically responds within 5-15 business days
5. **Sessions:** Authorization covers 30-36 TMS sessions

## Michigan Provider Network

BCN members have access to TMS providers across Michigan:

- **Southeast Michigan (Detroit metro):** Henry Ford Health, Beaumont, and numerous private TMS clinics
- **Grand Rapids:** Spectrum Health TMS program
- **Ann Arbor:** University of Michigan Health System
- **Lansing:** Sparrow Health System
- **Flint, Saginaw, Traverse City:** Regional TMS availability

## Cost Sharing

- **In-network:** Specialist copay or coinsurance after deductible
- **BCN Medicare Advantage:** $30-$50 specialist copay per visit
- **BCN Commercial:** Deductible + coinsurance, typically 20%
- **Out-of-network:** Higher cost sharing, may not be covered in full

## BCN Medicare Advantage (Medicare Plus Blue)

If you have BCN's Medicare Advantage plan (Medicare Plus Blue PPO or PMD), TMS is covered with prior authorization. Your out-of-pocket is limited to your plan's annual maximum.

## Tips for BCN Members

- Confirm the TMS clinic is in-network with BCN specifically (not just BCBS Michigan)
- Ask your psychiatrist to document every medication you've tried, including augmentation strategies
- BCN uses eviCore for some behavioral health authorizations — your clinic knows the process
- Keep copies of all authorization approvals and denial letters
