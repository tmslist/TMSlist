## Does Medicaid cover TMS?

Medicaid coverage for TMS therapy varies significantly by state. Unlike Medicare (which has a national coverage policy for TMS), Medicaid is administered at the state level, and each state's Medicaid program makes its own coverage decisions.

As of 2026, a growing number of state Medicaid programs cover TMS for treatment-resistant depression, but coverage is not universal.

## States with Medicaid TMS coverage

The following states currently provide TMS coverage through their Medicaid programs (list not exhaustive — check your state for current status):

- **California (Medi-Cal):** Covers TMS with prior authorization after 2+ medication failures
- **New York:** Covers TMS for treatment-resistant depression
- **Texas:** Limited coverage; requires extensive documentation
- **Florida:** Covers through some managed care plans
- **Illinois:** Covers TMS with prior authorization
- **Ohio:** Covers through managed care organizations

Coverage policies change frequently. Contact your state Medicaid office or managed care plan directly for the most current information.

## Typical eligibility requirements

When states do cover TMS, they generally require:

1. **Diagnosis:** Major Depressive Disorder (MDD) confirmed by a psychiatrist
2. **Medication failures:** 2-4 adequate trials of antidepressants at therapeutic doses for adequate duration (usually 6-8 weeks each)
3. **Documentation:** Clinical records showing medication trials, doses, duration, and reason for failure
4. **Prior authorization:** Required in virtually all state Medicaid programs
5. **Provider requirements:** TMS must be administered by a licensed provider at an approved facility

## How to get authorization

1. **Confirm your plan covers TMS:** Call the number on your Medicaid card and ask specifically about TMS therapy for depression
2. **Get a referral:** Most Medicaid plans require a psychiatrist referral
3. **Document medication history:** Your provider should compile records of all prior medication trials
4. **Submit prior authorization:** Your TMS clinic typically handles this, but stay involved — follow up on timelines
5. **Appeal if denied:** Medicaid denials can be appealed. Request the specific reason for denial and address it in your appeal

## Medicaid managed care vs fee-for-service

Many states deliver Medicaid through managed care organizations (MCOs). Your coverage depends on which MCO you're enrolled in:

- **MCO plans** may have different TMS policies than the state's fee-for-service program
- Some MCOs have carved out behavioral health to separate companies
- Call your specific plan, not just "Medicaid," to get accurate coverage information

## Cost if Medicaid doesn't cover TMS

If your state's Medicaid program doesn't cover TMS:

- Ask the TMS clinic about sliding-scale fees or payment plans
- Some clinics participate in manufacturer-sponsored access programs
- Clinical trials may offer TMS at no cost
- Contact NeuroStar's patient assistance program for potential options

## Tips for Medicaid patients

- Start the prior authorization process early — Medicaid approvals can take 2-4 weeks
- Keep copies of all medication records and psychiatrist notes
- If denied, always appeal — many initial denials are reversed on appeal
- Ask your TMS clinic if they have experience with Medicaid patients (some clinics don't accept Medicaid)
