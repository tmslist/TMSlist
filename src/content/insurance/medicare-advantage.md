## Medicare Advantage vs Original Medicare for TMS

Both Original Medicare (Parts A & B) and Medicare Advantage (Part C) cover TMS therapy for treatment-resistant depression. However, the process differs:

**Original Medicare:** Covers TMS under Part B as an outpatient procedure. You pay 20% coinsurance after meeting your Part B deductible. No prior authorization required for most claims.

**Medicare Advantage:** Covers TMS but may require prior authorization, have specific network requirements, and different cost sharing. Each MA plan sets its own rules within CMS guidelines.

## Key differences

| Factor | Original Medicare | Medicare Advantage |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|
| Prior authorization | Usually not required | Often required |
| Network restrictions | Any Medicare-accepting provider | Must use plan's network |
| Cost sharing | 20% coinsurance after deductible | Varies by plan (copay or coinsurance) |
| Referral needed | No | Some HMO plans require it |
| Annual out-of-pocket max | No limit on Part B | Required by law ($8,850 in 2026) |

## Medicare Advantage authorization process

1. **Get a psychiatrist assessment** documenting treatment-resistant depression
2. **Document medication failures** (minimum 2 adequate trials, usually from different classes)
3. **Prior authorization request** submitted by your TMS clinic to your MA plan
4. **Timeline:** 7-14 days for standard review; 72 hours for expedited
5. **Authorization typically covers** a full 36-session course

## Cost for MA members

Typical out-of-pocket costs:
- **Specialist copay plans:** $30-$50 per TMS session
- **Coinsurance plans:** 20% of allowed amount per session
- **Annual max:** All TMS costs count toward your plan's out-of-pocket maximum
- **Example:** 36 sessions x $40 copay = $1,440 total out-of-pocket

## Tips for Medicare Advantage members

- **Verify network status** before starting — out-of-network TMS clinics may cost 2-3x more or not be covered at all
- **Start prior auth early** — MA plans can take longer than Original Medicare to process
- **Keep medication records** — the more detailed your history, the smoother the authorization
- **Check your plan's out-of-pocket max** — if you've already had significant medical expenses this year, TMS may be mostly covered
- **Compare to Original Medicare:** If you're considering switching during open enrollment, compare TMS coverage under both options
