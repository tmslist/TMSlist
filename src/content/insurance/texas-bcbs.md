## BCBS Texas TMS coverage

Blue Cross Blue Shield of Texas covers TMS therapy for treatment-resistant depression under its commercial, state employee (ERS/TRS), and Federal Employee Health Benefit (FEHB) plans. Texas has a large and growing TMS provider network, particularly in the major metro areas.

## Eligibility

BCBS Texas follows the national BCBS medical policy:

1. **MDD diagnosis** by a treating psychiatrist
2. **Failed 2+ adequate antidepressant trials** from different pharmacological classes
3. **Adequate trial:** Therapeutic dose for at least 6 weeks
4. **Prior authorization** required before treatment
5. **In-network provider** strongly recommended for best benefits

## Covered services

- Up to 36 sessions of rTMS (standard or theta burst)
- All FDA-cleared TMS devices
- Maintenance sessions: covered under some plan designs — verify with BCBS Texas

## Texas TMS provider landscape

Texas is one of the largest TMS markets in the US:
- **Dallas-Fort Worth:** 40+ TMS providers
- **Houston:** 35+ providers
- **San Antonio:** 15+ providers
- **Austin:** 15+ providers
- **El Paso, Lubbock, Corpus Christi:** Growing availability

## Cost sharing

- **Blue Choice PPO:** 20% coinsurance after deductible (in-network)
- **Blue Advantage HMO:** Specialist copay ($40-$60 per session)
- **State employee (ERS) plans:** Favorable copays, typically $30-$50
- **Teacher (TRS) plans:** Varies by selected plan tier

## Prior authorization process

1. TMS clinic submits authorization request to BCBS Texas
2. Include: medication trial history, PHQ-9 scores, psychiatrist evaluation
3. Typical response: 5-10 business days
4. Authorization covers full treatment course (36 sessions)

## Texas-specific considerations

- Texas has no state mandate requiring TMS coverage — it falls under general mental health parity requirements
- If you have a self-funded employer plan administered by BCBS Texas, the employer (not BCBS) makes coverage decisions
- Texas Medicaid coverage for TMS is limited — most Medicaid managed care plans in Texas don't cover TMS yet
- Texas workers' comp may cover TMS for work-related depression (see your claims examiner)

## Tips

- Verify your plan covers TMS before scheduling — call the number on your BCBS Texas card
- Ask the TMS clinic if they specifically contract with BCBS Texas (vs other BCBS plans)
- If denied, file a formal appeal within 180 days and request peer-to-peer review
- Texas Department of Insurance (TDI) handles complaints if you believe coverage was wrongly denied
