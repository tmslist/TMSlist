## BCBS coverage for TMS

Most Blue Cross Blue Shield plans cover TMS for treatment-resistant depression. BCBS is the largest health insurer in the US, but coverage details vary significantly by state (each BCBS company operates independently).

## General requirements across BCBS plans

While specifics vary, most BCBS plans require:

- **Diagnosis**: Major Depressive Disorder (single episode or recurrent)
- **Failed medications**: 2-4 adequate antidepressant trials
- **Psychotherapy**: Some plans require a history of therapy in addition to medication
- **Prior authorization**: Required in almost all BCBS plans
- **In-network provider**: Using an in-network TMS clinic significantly reduces your cost

## State-by-state differences

BCBS isn't one company — it's 35+ independent companies. Some notable differences:

- **BCBS Illinois, Texas, Florida**: Generally straightforward approval with 2 failed medications
- **BCBS Massachusetts (Blue Cross MA)**: May require 3-4 failed medications and a peer review
- **Anthem BCBS**: Covers TMS but often requires 4 failed medications (stricter than most)
- **Horizon BCBS (New Jersey)**: Requires prior auth and has a specific TMS policy with detailed criteria

Check your specific plan's Evidence of Coverage document, or call the number on the back of your card.

## What you'll pay

- **In-network with good coverage**: $0-$2,000 total (after deductible, copay per session)
- **Out-of-network**: Significantly more — potentially $5,000-$10,000 depending on your out-of-network benefits
- **High-deductible plan**: You may pay full cost until meeting your deductible, then coinsurance applies

Always get a pre-authorization AND a cost estimate from the clinic before starting treatment.

## If you're denied

BCBS denials are usually for one of three reasons:
1. **Insufficient documentation** of failed medications — your psychiatrist can provide additional records
2. **Plan exclusion** — some older or grandfathered plans exclude TMS. Check your plan documents.
3. **Not meeting the specific number of failed trials** — ask your psychiatrist to document all trials, including ones that were stopped due to side effects (those count)

You have the right to appeal. First-level appeals succeed about 40-50% of the time for TMS. If the first appeal fails, request an external review — an independent reviewer often overturns the decision.
