## Understanding the UHC/Optum relationship

UnitedHealthcare (UHC) is the insurance plan. Optum Behavioral Health manages mental health benefits for many UHC plans. When seeking TMS coverage, you may interact with Optum for prior authorization even though your insurance card says UnitedHealthcare.

## Coverage overview

UnitedHealthcare covers TMS therapy for treatment-resistant MDD through most commercial and Medicare Advantage plans. Optum's medical policy (effective 2024, updated annually) outlines specific criteria.

## Eligibility requirements

1. **Diagnosis:** Major Depressive Disorder, single episode or recurrent, moderate to severe
2. **Medication failures:** Minimum 2 adequate antidepressant trials
   - From different pharmacological classes
   - At adequate therapeutic doses for at least 8 weeks each
   - Failure defined as: inadequate response, intolerable side effects, or contraindication
3. **Current episode:** Active depression with PHQ-9 score documenting moderate-severe symptoms
4. **Psychotherapy:** Some plans require concurrent or prior psychotherapy engagement
5. **Provider:** Must be performed by or under supervision of a psychiatrist at an approved facility

## Authorization process

1. **Contact Optum Behavioral Health** (number on back of UHC card, behavioral health section)
2. **TMS clinic submits prior auth** with clinical documentation
3. **Required documentation:**
   - Comprehensive psychiatric evaluation
   - Medication trial history (drug names, doses, durations, outcomes)
   - Current PHQ-9 or HAM-D score
   - Treatment plan from treating psychiatrist
4. **Review timeline:** 5-15 business days
5. **Authorization period:** Typically covers full 36-session course

## Cost sharing

- **UHC Choice Plus PPO:** 20% coinsurance after deductible (in-network)
- **UHC Navigate HMO:** Specialist copay per session ($30-$60)
- **UHC Medicare Advantage:** Varies by plan; typically specialist copay
- **All plans:** TMS counts toward annual out-of-pocket maximum

## Tips for UHC/Optum members

- Call the **behavioral health number** on your card (not the medical number) for TMS questions
- Ask your TMS clinic if they have experience with Optum authorizations — the process differs from other insurers
- If initially denied, **peer-to-peer review** between your psychiatrist and Optum's reviewer often reverses denials
- UHC's online portal may not show TMS benefits clearly — call to confirm before assuming it's not covered
- Keep a copy of your authorization reference number and provide it at each session
