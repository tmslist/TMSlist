## Aetna's TMS policy

Aetna covers TMS under **Clinical Policy Bulletin (CPB) 0612**. The policy considers TMS "medically necessary" for treatment-resistant depression when specific criteria are met.

## Requirements

Aetna requires all of the following:

1. **Diagnosis**: Major Depressive Disorder (MDD), unipolar, non-psychotic
2. **Failed medications**: At least **4 adequate antidepressant trials** from at least 2 different classes
3. **Each trial**: Must have been at adequate dose for at least 6 weeks
4. **Current episode**: Must be moderate to severe (PHQ-9 score of 10 or higher)
5. **Device**: Must use an FDA-cleared TMS device

**Note**: Aetna's 4-medication requirement is stricter than many insurers (who require only 2). Make sure your psychiatrist documents all trials thoroughly.

## Authorization process

1. Your TMS clinic submits a precertification request
2. Include: psychiatric evaluation, medication history with dates/doses/outcomes, PHQ-9 score
3. Aetna typically responds within 5-10 business days
4. Approved for up to 36 sessions initially

## Cost

- **In-network**: Copay or coinsurance applies after deductible. Typical total out-of-pocket: $500-$3,000
- **Out-of-network**: Much higher. Check your out-of-network benefits before going this route

## Common issues

- **The 4-med requirement catches people off guard.** Make sure to count all antidepressants tried, including augmentation agents (lithium, Abilify, thyroid) — Aetna may count these
- **Aetna CVS Health merger** hasn't changed the TMS policy as of 2026
- **Aetna Medicare Advantage**: Follows standard Medicare TMS coverage, which typically requires only 2 failed medications (less strict than commercial Aetna)
