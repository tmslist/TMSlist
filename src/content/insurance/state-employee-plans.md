## Do state employee plans cover TMS?

Most state employee health plans cover TMS therapy for treatment-resistant depression, as they typically follow the same clinical guidelines as major commercial insurers. However, coverage details vary significantly by state and by which insurer administers the plan.

## How state plans work

State employee plans are negotiated between state governments and insurance carriers. Your coverage depends on:

1. **Which state you work for** (each state negotiates separately)
2. **Which carrier administers your plan** (BCBS, Aetna, UHC, Cigna, etc.)
3. **Which tier or plan level you selected** (PPO, HMO, HDHP)

The administering carrier's standard TMS medical policy usually applies.

## States with confirmed TMS coverage

State employee plans administered by major carriers generally cover TMS. Examples:

- **California (CalPERS):** Covers through BCBS, Kaiser, UHC, and Anthem plans
- **Texas (ERS):** Covers through BCBS of Texas and UHC
- **New York:** Covers through Empire BCBS and other participating carriers
- **Florida:** Covers through Florida Blue and Aetna plans
- **Illinois:** Covers through BCBS of Illinois
- **Pennsylvania (PEBTF):** Covers through multiple carriers

## How to check your specific plan

1. **Look at your insurance card** — identify the administering carrier (BCBS, Aetna, etc.)
2. **Call the behavioral health number** on your card
3. **Ask specifically:** "Does my plan cover repetitive transcranial magnetic stimulation (rTMS) for treatment-resistant depression?"
4. **Get details:** Prior auth requirements, in-network providers, cost sharing
5. **Request in writing:** Ask for a written summary of TMS coverage under your specific plan

## General eligibility criteria

Most state plans follow standard criteria:
- MDD diagnosis by a psychiatrist
- 2+ adequate antidepressant trials failed
- Prior authorization required
- Must use in-network TMS provider

## Cost sharing

State employee plans often have favorable cost sharing:
- **Copay plans:** $20-$50 per specialist visit (per TMS session)
- **Coinsurance plans:** 10-20% after deductible
- **Annual max:** TMS costs count toward out-of-pocket maximum (often $3,000-$6,000 for individual)

## Tips for state employees

- Your HR benefits coordinator can help clarify TMS coverage
- State employee plans sometimes have separate behavioral health carve-outs — make sure you contact the correct benefits administrator
- If your plan uses a BCBS carrier, their national TMS policy likely applies
- Open enrollment is your chance to switch to a plan with better behavioral health coverage if needed
