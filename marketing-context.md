# TMSlist Brand Voice Guide

## Brand Identity
- **Name**: TMS List
- **What we are**: The most comprehensive directory of TMS therapy providers in America (expanding internationally)
- **Who we serve**: Patients struggling with treatment-resistant depression, anxiety, OCD — and the clinics that treat them
- **Core promise**: We make finding the right TMS provider feel less overwhelming

## Voice Characteristics

### Tone: Warm authority
- We know this space deeply, but we never talk down to patients
- We're the trusted friend who happens to know a lot about TMS
- Direct without being cold. Empathetic without being saccharine

### Writing Rules
1. **Lead with what the patient cares about** — not what sounds impressive
2. **Use "you" not "patients" or "individuals"** — talk to a person
3. **Short sentences for impact. Longer ones when explaining something complex.**
4. **Name real things** — specific devices, specific conditions, specific numbers
5. **Skip the corporate hedge** — if we know something, say it plainly
6. **Acknowledge the hard part** — seeking mental health treatment takes courage. Don't gloss over it.

### Words We Use
- "find" not "navigate"
- "real" not "robust"  
- "works for" not "is suitable for"
- "costs" not "pricing structures"
- "talk to" not "consult with"
- "covered by" not "eligible for reimbursement under"

### Words We Don't Use
- delve, leverage, crucial, vital, holistic, comprehensive (unless literally comprehensive)
- "It's important to note" — just say the thing
- "Furthermore" / "Moreover" — start the sentence
- "Navigate your journey" — nobody says this in real life

### Emotional Register
- **Hopeful but honest**: TMS works for a lot of people. Not everyone. We say both.
- **Respectful of the struggle**: Getting to the point of searching for TMS means medications haven't worked. That's exhausting. We get it.
- **Action-oriented**: Every page should make the next step obvious

## Example Voice

**Before (AI-generic):**
> "It is crucial to leverage comprehensive insurance verification tools to navigate the complex landscape of TMS therapy coverage. Furthermore, patients should consider consulting with multiple providers to ensure optimal treatment outcomes."

**After (TMSlist voice):**
> "Before your first appointment, call your insurance company and ask one question: 'Do you cover TMS therapy for depression?' If they say yes, ask about prior authorization. Most plans need it. We've listed which insurances each clinic accepts — so you can filter for yours and skip the guesswork."
