1. The 5-Channel Patient Acquisition Framework

Every growing TMS practice relies on a diversified patient acquisition strategy. Relying on a single channel is risky — algorithm changes, referral fatigue, and market shifts can erode your pipeline overnight. The framework below distributes your effort across five complementary channels, each with distinct strengths, costs, and timelines. Use this as a strategic map, then dive into the channel-specific playbooks in subsequent sections.

Channel Overview

# Channel Estimated Lead Volume Cost Per Lead (CPL) Timeline to Results Best For
1 Physician Referrals 5–25 consults/month $0–$80 3–6 months High-intent, insurance-verified patients
2 Paid Search (Google Ads) 30–150 clicks/day $40–$120 2–4 weeks Immediate lead flow, keyword targeting
3 Organic SEO & Content 10–60 organic visits/day $5–$25 (content cost) 6–12 months Long-term compounding traffic, authority
4 Patient Reviews & Reputation Indirect — amplifies all channels $0–$20/review 1–3 months Trust signals, conversion rate lift
5 Direct-to-Consumer (DTC) Campaigns Varies by spend $60–$200 2–8 weeks Brand awareness, patient self-referrals
Strategic Note

Start with 2–3 channels simultaneously for the first 90 days. Physician referrals are the highest-quality leads but take time to build. Pair them with Google Ads for immediate volume while your referral network matures. Organic SEO is a long-term investment — begin content production now even if results are months away.

Channel 1 — Physician Referrals

Psychiatrists, primary care physicians (PCPs), therapists, and nurse practitioners are your highest-quality referral sources. A referred patient has a 3–5x higher consultation-to-treatment conversion rate compared to a cold ad click. The physician referral blueprint is covered in Section 3.

Channel 2 — Paid Search (Google Ads)

Google Ads provides the fastest path to patient leads. With proper keyword selection, ad copy, and landing page optimization, you can be generating qualified leads within two weeks of launching a campaign. The full Google Ads setup is in Section 2.

Channel 3 — Organic SEO & Content Marketing

Long-form blog articles, patient FAQs, provider guides, and TMS explainer content all serve to capture patients searching for answers online. Content published today continues to generate traffic for years — making this the highest-return investment over a 24-month horizon.

Channel 4 — Patient Reviews & Reputation

Online reviews on Google, Yelp, and Healthgrades are trust signals that influence every other channel. A practice with 50+ reviews averaging 4.8 stars converts 30–40% better than a comparable practice with fewer reviews. Section 5 covers systematic testimonial collection.

Channel 5 — Direct-to-Consumer (DTC) Campaigns

Social media advertising, community outreach, employer wellness partnerships, and patient education webinars all fall under DTC. These campaigns bypass physician gatekeepers and reach patients directly who may be suffering in silence.

Landing Page Requirements

Your Google Ads must point to a dedicated landing page — not your homepage. The landing page must include:

  • Hero headline matching the ad copy headline within 3 words
  • Sub-headline addressing the patient's primary pain point (e.g., "When medication isn't enough")
  • CTA button above the fold: "Schedule Free Consultation" or "Check My Insurance"
  • Insurance logos or "Most Major Insurance Accepted" badge
  • Social proof: star rating, review count, or patient photo
  • FAQ section addressing top objections (cost, safety, time commitment)
  • Phone number and click-to-call link in the header
  • Trust signals: FDA clearance mention, provider credentials, clinic photos
  • Form: Name, phone, email, and one qualifying question (e.g., "Have you tried medication for your depression?")
Landing Page Tip

Pages that load in under 3 seconds convert 32% better than slower pages. Compress images, use a CDN, and eliminate unnecessary scripts. A dedicated landing page should have zero navigation links to other pages — every path leads to the consultation booking.

Bidding Strategy

  • Initial 30 days: Manual CPC bidding — gives you control while you learn which keywords convert
  • Days 31–60: Switch to Target CPA (cost per acquisition) bidding, using your historical conversion data
  • Month 3+: Target ROAS (return on ad spend) at 3x or higher — meaning every dollar spent returns $3
  • Audience signals: Upload your existing patient email list as a Customer Match audience to target similar users
  • Location targeting: 15–25 mile radius around your clinic. Add surrounding zip codes where drive time is under 45 minutes
  • Schedule optimization: Run ads 7 days/week, but analyze which days/times generate consultations vs. just clicks. Pause low-performing time slots after 4 weeks of data

Follow-Up Cadence

Day 0 — Initial Outreach

Send email + physical letter simultaneously. Mail the letter to the practice address, email to the provider's known inbox.

Day 7 — First Follow-Up Call

Call the practice front desk. Ask for Dr. [Name]'s office manager or clinical assistant. Briefly introduce yourself and confirm receipt of the letter. Request a 10-minute call with the physician.

Day 14 — Second Email

Send a value-add email: share a relevant clinical study, a patient success story (de-identified), or an upcoming TMS education event. Keep it brief and provider-focused.

Day 30 — Lunch-and-Learn Invitation

Formally invite the physician to a lunch-and-learn at their office or a nearby restaurant. Offer to bring food. Present a 20-minute TMS overview with clinical data. See Section 3 of the companion playbook for full Lunch-and-Learn details.

Send a brief quarterly update: number of referrals received, patient outcomes, any new TMS protocols or insurance updates. Personalize — this is a relationship, not a blast.

Day 90 — Re-Engagement

If no referrals after 90 days, send a re-engagement message highlighting a new TMS protocol, a new clinical publication, or a patient outcome from a similar case type.

5. Patient Testimonial Collection & Deployment

Testimonials are the most persuasive element in your patient acquisition arsenal. A strong testimonial functions as peer validation — the closest digital equivalent to a word-of-mouth recommendation. This section covers the complete workflow: consent, collection, production, and deployment across every channel.

Consent Form Checklist

Before collecting any testimonial, ensure your practice has a compliant consent process. This checklist applies regardless of format (written, video, or audio):

  • Patient is 18 years of age or older and has legal capacity to consent
  • Patient has completed or is near completion of their TMS treatment course
  • Patient has been informed of how the testimonial will be used (website, social media, paid ads, print)
  • Patient has signed a written testimonial release form that includes: name (or anonymity preference), photo/video release, specific scope of use, right to withdraw consent, date and signature
  • Patient understands they will not receive compensation for the testimonial (or if compensated, this must be disclosed)
  • For video testimonials: a separate video-specific consent form is signed
  • For HIPAA-covered contexts: patient has been offered the option to use a pseudonym and has agreed to the chosen format
  • Consent form is filed in the patient's record
  • Marketing team or external agency has received written approval to use the content
Legal Note

Consult your healthcare attorney to ensure your testimonial consent forms comply with state medical board advertising rules, FTC endorsement guidelines, and your specific malpractice insurance requirements. Regulations vary by state.

How to Ask for Testimonials

The timing and method of your ask dramatically affects response rates. The best time to request a testimonial is at the moment of peak positive emotion — typically after session 15–20 (when patients start noticing improvement) or after completing the full course of treatment.

Script: In-Person Ask (Best Response Rate)

Provider: "[Patient Name], I have to tell you — it's been really gratifying to see how well you're responding to treatment. How are you feeling compared to when you started?"

[Patient shares positive experience]

We could do a short video, or even just a written quote — whatever you're comfortable with. And we always keep it completely confidential unless you want to share your name."

Video vs. Written Testimonials

Video Testimonials (Preferred)

  • Engagement: Video posts receive 2–3x more engagement than text on social media
  • Trust: Facial expressions and tone of voice convey authenticity that text cannot replicate
  • Versatility: One 60–90 second video can be repurposed into: Instagram Reel, Facebook post, YouTube short, website embed, Google Ads creative, and waiting room display
  • Production tip: Use natural light, a ring light or softbox, and film on a smartphone in 4K. No need for professional studio — authenticity beats polish

Written Testimonials

  • Ease: Lower barrier to entry — patients who won't film can still write
  • Specificity: Written testimonials can be curated for exact phrases and keywords (helpful for SEO and ad copy)
  • Use cases: Google Business Profile, Healthgrades, Yelp, website testimonial rotator, email signature, print materials
  • Formatting tip: Include a star rating with written testimonials on review platforms. A 5-star quote is more credible than a starless one

6. ROI Tracking Spreadsheet Layout

You cannot improve what you do not measure. This section provides a complete spreadsheet framework for tracking every patient acquisition channel by its financial performance. Use this template monthly to identify your best-performing channels, cut wasted spend, and allocate budget to maximize ROI.

Key Metrics Definitions

  • CPL (Cost Per Lead): Total channel spend ÷ number of leads generated
  • CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost): Total marketing spend ÷ number of patients who started TMS treatment
  • ROAS (Return on Ad Spend): Revenue generated ÷ ad spend. A ROAS of 3x means $3 in revenue for every $1 spent
  • Conversion Rate: Percentage of leads that convert to consultations, and consultations that convert to treatment starts
  • Average Revenue Per Patient: Include all revenue streams: TMS sessions, office visits, new patient evaluations
  • LTV (Lifetime Value): Total revenue from a patient over their entire relationship with your practice (includes maintenance sessions, referrals they send)
  • Payback Period: CAC ÷ monthly revenue per patient = how many months to recoup acquisition cost

7. Glossary of Key Terms

Use this glossary as a reference for understanding the marketing and business terminology used throughout this playbook. It is also a useful patient-facing resource — share it with prospective patients who are researching TMS options online.

CAC Customer Acquisition Cost. The total marketing and sales cost required to acquire one new patient. Calculated as total spend divided by number of new patients acquired in a given period. Includes advertising spend, staff time, materials, and referral program costs.
Churn Churn Rate. The percentage of patients who begin TMS treatment but do not complete the full course. High churn indicates patient dropout due to cost, side effects, inconvenience, or lack of perceived benefit. Target churn rate: below 15%.
Conversion Rate The percentage of visitors or leads who take a desired action. In TMS marketing, the most important conversion rates are: lead-to-consultation (how many inquiry submissions become booked appointments) and consult-to-start (how many consultations result in treatment initiation). Industry benchmark: 25–40% lead-to-consult; 50–65% consult-to-start.
CPL Cost Per Lead. The amount spent to generate one patient inquiry or lead. Calculated as total channel spend divided by number of leads generated. The lower the CPL, the more efficiently the channel generates interest. Target CPL varies by market but typically ranges $40–$120 for TMS Google Ads campaigns.
CTR Click-Through Rate. The percentage of people who see an ad (impressions) and click on it. Calculated as clicks ÷ impressions × 100. A higher CTR indicates more compelling ad copy or better audience targeting. Industry benchmark for TMS Google Search ads: 5–10%.
DTC Direct-to-Consumer. Marketing strategies that reach and engage patients directly, bypassing physician gatekeepers. Examples include social media ads, content marketing, patient education webinars, and employer wellness programs. DTC is essential for TMS because most patients with treatment-resistant depression do not know TMS exists.
Evergreen Content Content that remains relevant and continues to generate traffic long after it is published. Examples for TMS: "What is TMS therapy?", "Does insurance cover TMS?", "TMS vs. ECT." Evergreen content is the foundation of organic SEO because it accumulates search traffic over time without requiring ongoing promotion.
Funnel Marketing Funnel. The journey a prospective patient takes from first awareness of your practice to booking a consultation and beginning treatment. The standard model: Awareness → Interest → Consideration → Intent → Evaluation → Booking → Treatment. Each stage requires different content and engagement strategies.
Funnel Stage A specific point in the patient's journey through the marketing funnel. Top-of-funnel (TOFU) content educates and builds awareness (e.g., "What is TMS?"). Middle-of-funnel (MOFU) content addresses objections and builds consideration (e.g., "TMS vs. medication"). Bottom-of-funnel (BOFU) content drives action (e.g., "Book your free consultation").
Impression The number of times an ad or piece of content is displayed to a user. Impressions are not clicks — they measure visibility. A high impression count with low click-through indicates targeting or creative issues. Impressions are used to calculate CTR (clicks ÷ impressions).
Inbound Inbound Marketing. Marketing strategy that attracts patients to your practice through valuable content and experiences rather than interruptive advertising. Examples: blog articles that answer patient questions, SEO-optimized landing pages, patient education webinars. Inbound leads typically have higher conversion rates than outbound leads.
Keyword Difficulty A score (typically 0–100) indicating how hard it is to rank organically for a given keyword. High difficulty keywords (60+) are dominated by established websites and require significant SEO investment. Low difficulty keywords (under 30) offer faster ranking opportunities. For local TMS practices, long-tail local keywords (e.g., "TMS therapy Austin TX") typically have lower difficulty.
LTV Lifetime Value. The total revenue a patient generates for your practice over their entire relationship with you. Includes initial treatment course, maintenance sessions, additional services, and referrals they send. A patient with an LTV of $8,000 can sustain a CAC of $500 and remain profitable. Knowing your LTV is essential for setting acquisition budget.
MQL Marketing Qualified Lead. A lead who has engaged with your marketing — filling out a form, downloading a guide, or calling the clinic — and meets basic criteria for being a potential patient (e.g., location, insurance, diagnosis). MQLs are passed to the sales/consultation team for follow-up.
Organic Search Unpaid search engine results. When someone types a query into Google and clicks a non-ad result, that is organic traffic. Organic search is driven by SEO (Search Engine Optimization) and builds over time. A well-optimized TMS website can rank for hundreds of keywords and generate consistent, free patient inquiries indefinitely.
Paid Search Search engine advertising where you pay for each click on your ad. The most common platform is Google Ads (formerly AdWords). You bid on keywords; your ad appears above or below organic results; you pay per click. Paid search provides immediate traffic and leads but stops when your budget runs out — unlike organic SEO which compounds over time.
Quality Score Google's rating (1–10) of the quality and relevance of your ads, keywords, and landing pages. Higher Quality Scores lead to lower CPCs and better ad positions. Factors: expected CTR, ad relevance, and landing page experience. Target Quality Score of 7 or above for best efficiency.
Referral A patient directed to your practice by another healthcare provider (physician referral) or by an existing patient (patient referral). Physician referrals are initiated by a doctor sending a patient to your TMS clinic. Patient referrals (WOMM) are initiated when existing patients recommend your practice to friends or family. Referrals convert at 3–5x the rate of cold leads.
Remarketing Also called Retargeting. A digital advertising technique that shows ads to people who have previously visited your website or engaged with your content. Because these users have already shown interest, remarketing typically has 3–5x higher conversion rates than standard display advertising. Implemented via tracking pixels (e.g., Google Ads remarketing tag).
ROI Return on Investment. The net profit generated from a marketing activity relative to its cost. Calculated as (Revenue − Cost) ÷ Cost. A marketing channel with an ROI of 200% generates $2 in profit for every $1 spent. ROI is the ultimate measure of marketing effectiveness.
ROAS Return on Ad Spend. Specifically measures the revenue generated per dollar spent on advertising. Calculated as Revenue from Ads ÷ Ad Spend. Target ROAS varies by business model and margins — for TMS, a ROAS of 3x–5x is considered healthy; 8x+ is excellent. Note: ROAS does not account for profit margins, only revenue.
SaaS Software as a Service. A software delivery model where applications are hosted in the cloud and accessed via subscription. In healthcare marketing, CRM platforms (HubSpot, Salesforce), email marketing tools (Mailchimp, Klaviyo), and scheduling software are often SaaS products. TMS List uses SaaS tools for patient acquisition analytics and CRM.
SEO Search Engine Optimization. The practice of improving your website to increase its visibility in organic (non-paid) search engine results. Includes on-page SEO (keyword-optimized content, meta tags, headers), off-page SEO (backlinks, citations), and technical SEO (site speed, mobile usability, structured data). For TMS practices, local SEO (Google Business Profile optimization, local citations) is especially important.
Session A group of user interactions with your website that take place within a given time frame. In Google Analytics, a single session can include multiple page views, events, and conversions. Sessions are the foundational unit of website traffic measurement. High session counts with low conversion rates indicate traffic quality issues.
UTM Urchin Tracking Module. A standardized text code added to a URL to track the performance of marketing campaigns in Google Analytics. UTM parameters identify the source (e.g., google), medium (e.g., cpc), campaign (e.g., spring_tms_promo), and content (e.g., ad_variant_a). Always use UTM parameters on every link in every campaign so you can accurately attribute leads and conversions to the correct channel.
WOMM Word-of-Mouth Marketing.Patient-driven referrals where existing patients recommend your practice to people in their personal network. WOMM is the most trusted form of marketing — recommendations from friends and family outperform all other advertising channels in consumer trust. Patient testimonials, referral incentive programs, and exceptional treatment experiences all fuel WOMM.
